# ✦ ROSEMARY LEGOH: OMNICHANNEL DIGITAL BLUEPRINT ✦
### *The Luxury User Journey — From Instagram Discovery to Conversion*

---

## 🎯 STRATEGIC PREMISE

The Rosemary Legoh digital ecosystem is not a storefront. It is a **curated gallery experience** — each touchpoint is designed to deepen desire, not accelerate transaction. The objective is to make the customer feel they are *gaining access*, not simply *making a purchase.*

---

## 🗺️ PART 1: THE OMNICHANNEL USER JOURNEY MAP

### Stage 1 — Discovery (Instagram)
**Platform Role:** The Prestige Window  
**User Mindset:** Browsing, not yet shopping

| Touchpoint | Content Type | Design Principle |
|---|---|---|
| Feed Post | Editorial image of the RL Fold | Cinematic, no overt CTA |
| Reel | 10-second structural close-up of the fold geometry | Slow-motion, ambient score |
| Stories | Behind-the-scenes visit to the Solo weaver | Raw, intimate, vertical format |
| Bio Link | Single link → Waitlist Landing Page or Website | No Linktree clutter |
| Highlight: "The Fold" | Curated 6-story explanation of RL Fold origins | Acts as a permanent primer |
| Highlight: "Our Patrons" | Reposts from VIP customers wearing pieces | Social proof without mass-market feel |

**Friction Budget:** Zero. The aesthetic must do all the work. No aggressive CTAs, no discount teasers.

---

### Stage 2 — Interest (Instagram → Pre-Qualification Layer)
**Trigger:** User taps the bio link or swipes up on a Story CTA ("See the full process →")

**Option A — WhatsApp Entry:**
> A Story posts: *"New drop arriving. If you'd like early access, send us a message."*  
> Pre-written WhatsApp deep link auto-populates: *"Hi, I'd like to be added to the Rosemary Legoh waitlist."*  
> A human (or semi-automated flow) responds within 2 hours with: curated PDF lookbook + a single qualifying question ("Which silhouette resonates with you most?")

**Option B — Website Landing Page:**
> Minimal waitlist form: Name + Email + Instagram Handle  
> On submission: Auto-response email delivers the "Brand Manifesto" PDF — the RL Fold story, the weaver partnership, and the upcoming drop details.

**Design Principle:** This stage is a **velvet rope**, not a funnel. Friction is intentional — only motivated buyers proceed.

### Stage 4 — Conversion (WhatsApp Checkout or Website Cart)
**Recommended Hybrid Model for the Indonesian Market:**

| Channel | Best For | Experience Design |
|---|---|---|
| WhatsApp | VIP / Waitlist clients | Personal, concierge-level; no payment friction |
| Website | New organic traffic | Streamlined; 2-click checkout max |

**WhatsApp Conversion Script:**  
> "Your reserved piece: [Product Name], Size [M], IDR [Price]. To confirm, reply with your delivery address — we'll send payment details directly. Your piece is held for 24 hours."

The 24-hour hold creates real scarcity while the personal channel creates intimacy. This is not automated bulk messaging — it is designed to feel like a personal stylist.

## 🖥️ PART 2: PRODUCT PAGE ARCHITECTURE

A Rosemary Legoh product page must answer one implicit question the luxury buyer always asks: *"Why is this worth it?"* — before they think to ask it.

### Page Structure (Top to Bottom)

---

**[1] HERO SECTION**
- Full-bleed video (autoplay, muted): 8-second clip of the RL Fold being draped — hands only, no face
- On top: Product Name in minimal serif typeface (e.g., "Outer No. 04 — The Surakarta")
- Subtext: Limited edition. 30 pieces only. Drop 02 / 2026.
- Price displayed immediately — no mystery. Luxury does not hide its price.

---

**[2] THE FOLD EXPLANATION MODULE**
> This is the brand's most differentiating section and must receive more visual estate than any other block.

- **Headline:** *"What is the RL Fold?"*
- **Format:** Embedded 60-second vertical video (filmed like a documentary micro-clip)
  - Segment 1 (0–15s): Archival footage of Indonesian *wiron* pleating on traditional batik
  - Segment 2 (15–40s): The designer's hands demonstrating the asymmetric fold geometry
  - Segment 3 (40–60s): The finished piece on a model — the fold in motion as she walks
- **Caption Block (below video):**  
  > *"The RL Fold is a structural construction technique that bridges traditional Javanese pleating with modernist architectural principles. No two folds are identical. Each is finished by hand."*
- **Supporting Diagram:** Optional — a clean line-drawing of the fold's geometry (SVG preferred) with labeled construction points: *Wiron Origin / Asymmetric Break / Structural Collar / Final Drape Point*

---

**[3] FABRIC & PROVENANCE SECTION**
- A single photograph: The weaver at their loom in Solo or Pekalongan
- Caption: *"This textile was woven by [Artisan Name] in Pekalongan, Central Java. Warp: hand-spun cotton. Weft: 14-count silk blend. Commissioned exclusively for Rosemary Legoh."*
- Short block of copy (max 80 words) on the exclusivity of the motif

---

**[4] PRODUCT DETAILS (ACCORDION/EXPANDABLE)**
- Sizing: Size guide with body measurement charts (not vanity sizes)
- Care: Hand-wash instructions with specific temperature guidance for structural integrity of the fold
- Construction: Number of hand-finished points, fold construction hours per piece
- Materials: Full composition (%, GSM weight, dye process)

---

**[5] EDITORIAL GALLERY**
- 5 images: Mix of studio (white background for clarity) + location (Jakarta art gallery or interior for context)
- Include at minimum: one close-up of fold geometry, one full-body on model, one flat lay with ruler scale for size reference
- No lifestyle filler — every image earns its place by showing the garment's construction or context

**[7] CTA — CONVERSION ACTION**
- **Primary CTA:** `[ Reserve via WhatsApp ]` — sends pre-written message with product name and size

## 🔴 PART 3: SCARCITY UI DESIGN SYSTEM

Scarcity UI is a trust-based tool, not a manipulation tool. It must be **accurate, visible, and restrained.** Any false scarcity destroys the brand's core promise of authenticity.

### Scarcity Tier Framework

| Inventory Level | UI Display | Visual Treatment |
|---|---|---|
| 30–16 pieces | No count shown | Silence builds normalcy |
| 15–6 pieces | *"Limited — [X] pieces remaining in this drop"* | Neutral grey text, small font |
| 5–3 pieces | *"Almost gone — only [X] pieces left"* | Amber dot indicator + italic text |
| 2 pieces | *"Final 2 pieces"* | Red dot + bold weight |
| 1 piece | *"Last piece — reserved for 24 hours upon inquiry"* | Red dot + border highlight on CTA block |
| 0 pieces | Product page becomes a **Archive Entry** | CTA changes to "Join Waitlist for Drop 03" |

**Design Specification:**
- Scarcity copy sits directly above the CTA block — never in the hero or above the fold explanation
- Font: Same typeface as body copy — no urgency styling that clashes with the aesthetic
- No countdown timers. Timers are mass-market psychology. Rosemary Legoh uses **unit scarcity**, not **time scarcity**
- WhatsApp CTA carries a hold note: *"We hold your selected piece for 24 hours upon message."* This is the only time constraint — and it is real

### Archive Page (Sold Out State)
When a drop sells out, the product page does not disappear. It transforms into an **Archive Entry:**
- Hero image remains
- Copy changes to: *"Drop 02 — Sold Out. All 30 pieces found their homes."*
- Fold Explanation video remains as permanent brand content
- A link to the next drop waitlist is added below
- A "View All Archive" link is added to the nav

**Strategic Purpose:** Archive pages build social proof over time and act as SEO assets. A sold-out archive is proof of desirability.

## 📱 PART 4: MOBILE-FIRST UI PRINCIPLES

Over 85% of Indonesian luxury discovery occurs on mobile. The entire UX must be designed mobile-first.

| Principle | Implementation |
|---|---|
| **One-thumb navigation** | CTA buttons min. 48px height; placed in bottom 30% of screen |
| **Load speed** | Hero video loads at 720p max on mobile; images served in WebP format |
| **No horizontal scroll** | All grids collapse to single column below 768px breakpoint |
| **WhatsApp deep link** | Opens native WhatsApp app, not web — use `wa.me/` URL scheme |
| **Typography scaling** | Minimum 16px body copy on mobile; headers scale down proportionally |
| **Fold video** | Auto-plays on Story/Reel format; product page video requires tap to play on mobile (respects autoplay restrictions) |
