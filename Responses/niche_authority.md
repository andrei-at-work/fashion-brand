# ✦ NICHE AUTHORITY & COMMUNITY VOICE ✦
### *Rosemary Legoh — Community Management Playbook*
#### *Prepared by: Community Manager, Boutique Heritage Brand Practice*

---

## STRATEGIC BRIEF

**Brand:** Rosemary Legoh
**Audience:** Urban Indonesian women, 25–42, Jakarta / Bandung / Surabaya
**Core Value Proposition:** Modern Wastra — where Architectural Innovation meets Indonesian textile heritage
**Community Positioning:** Not a customer base. A *Patronage*.

> The goal of this playbook is not to build a following. It is to cultivate a **circle** — a small, self-selecting group of women who feel they *discovered* Rosemary Legoh before the world did. That feeling of early belonging is the most powerful retention tool a boutique brand possesses.

---

## PART I: THE COMMUNITY VOICE ARCHITECTURE

*The "Community Voice" is the consistent personality, tone, and language that Rosemary Legoh uses across every touchpoint — captions, DMs, story replies, WhatsApp broadcasts, and in-person events.*

---

### THE VOICE PILLARS

| Pillar | What It Is | What It Is Not |
|---|---|---|
| **Informed Intimacy** | Speaking like a knowledgeable friend who happens to know a master weaver | Lecturing. Explaining. Condescending. |
| **Quiet Confidence** | Asserting the value of The RL Fold without ever defending it | Boasting. Comparison. Over-explaining. |
| **Cultural Reverence** | Honoring the *ibu-ibu* artisans and their craft as the true authority | Tokenizing. "Exotic"-izing. Performative. |
| **Selective Inclusion** | Making the community feel chosen, not marketed to | FOMO manipulation. False urgency. Spam. |

---

### THE TONE CALIBRATION SCALE

Think of the voice as sitting at a precise point on each of these scales:

### THE VOCABULARY GLOSSARY

These are the *owned words* of Rosemary Legoh. Use them consistently until they become synonymous with the brand.

| Owned Word | Meaning in Context | Usage Example |
|---|---|---|
| **The Fold** | The RL Fold structural signature — the brand's core innovation | *"This is The Fold at its most geometric."* |
| **Patron** | What we call our customers — never "buyer," "follower," or "customer" | *"For our Patrons in the waiting room..."* |
| **The Inner Circle** | The waitlist / VIP community | *"The Inner Circle gets first access."* |
| **Wastra** | Indonesian textiles — always used with reverence, never as mere decoration | *"The Wastra in this piece comes from a single loom in Pekalongan."* |
| **The House** | Rosemary Legoh as an institution | *"The House is releasing only 30 pieces."* |
| **A Drop** | A new collection release | *"The next Drop is being hand-finished now."* |
| **Architectural** | The design philosophy — structure-first, motif-second | *"An architectural take on traditional parang."* |

## PART II: ARTISAN STORYTELLING TEMPLATES

*Behind-the-scenes content is the most trust-generating content a heritage brand can produce. These templates give the community a feeling of privileged access — as if they are watching the craft happen before it becomes commerce.*

---

### STORY FORMAT 1 — "THE MAKER'S HAND"
*Platform: Instagram Stories / Reels. Frequency: Once per production cycle.*

**Visual Direction:** Close-up video or photo of artisan hands working. No face required — hands *are* the story. Natural light. No filters. No text overlay beyond a minimal caption.

**Caption Template:**

> *"Ibu [First Name] has been folding fabric for [X] years in [City]. She learned [technique] from her mother, who learned it from hers.*
>
> *The [specific motif/technique — e.g., parang diagonal] you see being formed here will become The Fold on [Drop Name]. It takes her [X hours] per piece.*
>
> *There will be [number] of these in the world.*
>
> *— Rosemary"*

**Why It Works:** The personal sign-off from "Rosemary" (not the brand account) collapses the distance between founder and community. The scarcity fact at the end is stated, not sold.

---

### STORY FORMAT 2 — "THE MATERIAL DIARY"
*Platform: Instagram Feed (carousel). Frequency: Once per collection.*

**Structure:** A 5-slide carousel that walks from raw material to finished piece.

- **Slide 1:** The fabric bolt — uncut, unstructured. Caption: *"Before The Fold, there is the cloth."*
- **Slide 2:** The motif detail. Caption: *"[Motif name] from [region]. Woven on a [loom type]. [Specific fact about the weave count or dye process]."*
- **Slide 3:** The cutting table. Caption: *"Every pattern piece is cut by hand. The RL Fold requires a bias-cut margin of [measurement] — no machine can hold this tolerance."*
- **Slide 4:** Mid-construction detail of The Fold. Caption: *"This is the moment. This is why it cannot be copied."*
- **Slide 5:** Finished garment on a stand or body. Caption: *"[Drop Name]. [Number] pieces. Available to The Inner Circle first."*

---

### STORY FORMAT 3 — "THE ORIGIN VISIT"
*Platform: Instagram Reels or YouTube Shorts. Frequency: Once per season (when visiting artisan partners).*

**Narrative Arc:** Rosemary (or a trusted brand representative) visits the workshop. The video is *observational*, not promotional. No scripted lines. Authentic reactions.

**Caption Template:**

> *"We drove [X hours] from [city] to reach Ibu [Name]'s workshop. She has been our partner for [time period]. Her loom produces [X meters] per day.*
>
> *She showed us a new way to achieve the rigidity The Fold requires — a technique she developed herself. We asked if we could use it exclusively. She said yes.*
>
> *This is what it means to build a House, not just a brand.*
>
> *— Rosemary Legoh"*

---

### STORY FORMAT 4 — "THE IMPERFECTION NOTE"
*Platform: Instagram Stories or WhatsApp broadcast. Frequency: When applicable (and it will be — embrace it).*

*This is the most underused and most powerful format. When a batch is delayed, a motif deviates slightly, or production yields fewer pieces than planned — tell the community before they ask.*

**Template:**

> *"A note from The House.*
>
> *The [Drop Name] batch was meant to deliver 30 pieces. It will deliver 24.*
>
> *Ibu [Name] identified a tension inconsistency in 6 pieces during final pressing. They did not hold The Fold to our standard. They will not be released.*
>
> *The 24 remaining pieces are perfect. The Inner Circle waitlist will be notified in order.*
>
> *We would rather release less than release wrong.*
>
> *— Rosemary"*

**Why It Works:** Radical transparency in a sea of polished brand communications is *disarming*. It transforms a setback into a proof-of-standard. Patrons will share this unprompted.

## PART III: LEADING STYLING DISCUSSIONS

*The community should not just admire Rosemary Legoh pieces — they should style them, discuss them, and teach each other. The Community Manager's role is to be the *host* of these conversations, not the broadcaster.*

---

### THE THREE DISCUSSION FORMATS

---

#### FORMAT A — "THE STYLE CHALLENGE"
*Platform: Instagram Stories (Poll + Submission). Frequency: Monthly.*

**Mechanics:**
1. Post a photo of a piece from the current or past drop.
2. Ask a specific styling question — not *"How would you wear this?"* (too broad) but a constrained creative problem.
3. Collect submissions via DM or Stories tag.
4. Feature the best in a "Patron Styling" highlight — named and credited.

**Example Prompts:**

- *"The Fold works best when the inner is minimal. Show us your minimal. Tag us."*
- *"Three cities, three ways: How does a Patron in Jakarta style this differently from Surabaya? We want to see both."*
- *"The boardroom or the gallery opening? You decide. Show us the shoes."*

**Community Manager Response Protocol:**
- Reply to *every* submission within 24 hours.
- Do not just say "Love this!" — reference *one specific detail*. Example: *"The way you let the fold fall open — that's exactly how it was designed to move. Perfect."*

---

#### FORMAT B — "THE WASTRA DIALOGUE"
*Platform: Instagram Live or WhatsApp Group broadcast + reply thread. Frequency: Once per season.*

**Purpose:** Position Rosemary Legoh as the intellectual center of the Modern Wastra conversation — not just a seller of it.

**Structure (60 minutes):**

| Time | Segment |
|---|---|
| 0–10 min | Rosemary introduces one textile tradition (e.g., *kawung* geometry, *parang* diagonal logic) |
| 10–25 min | How that tradition is expressed *differently* in The RL Fold vs. conventional applications |
| 25–45 min | Open styling Q&A — Patrons submit styling dilemmas live |
| 45–60 min | "The Next Fold" — a brief, non-committal preview of the next collection's material inspiration |

**Community Manager Role During Live:**
- Pin key quotes from Rosemary in the comments.
- Acknowledge Patrons by name when they ask questions.
- Save the replay. Clip the 3 best moments for Reels within 48 hours.

---

#### FORMAT C — "THE WEAR REPORT"
*Platform: Instagram Close Friends Story or WhatsApp Broadcast. Frequency: 10–14 days after each Drop ships.*

**Mechanics:** Message every Patron from the last Drop personally (or via segmented broadcast):

> *"Satu pertanyaan kecil — sudah dipakai ke mana The Fold pertama kalinya?*
> *(One small question — where did The Fold go on its first outing?)"*

Collect responses. Curate the most interesting 5–8 into a "Wear Report" — a story format that maps where Rosemary Legoh pieces have appeared in the world.

**Why It Works:** It makes the Patron feel their experience *matters to the record*. It also generates authentic, location-tagged social proof without asking for a formal review.

## PART IV: THE PATRON EXPERIENCE — MAKING CUSTOMERS FEEL LIKE INNER CIRCLE MEMBERS

*This is the operational architecture of exclusivity. It is not fabricated scarcity or false urgency — it is a genuine system designed to make every Patron feel that their relationship with Rosemary Legoh is personal, earned, and rare.*

---

### THE PATRON JOURNEY MAP

```
AWARENESS          DISCOVERY          FIRST PURCHASE       PATRONAGE
     │                  │                   │                  │
     ▼                  ▼                   ▼                  ▼
Sees content      Joins waitlist      Receives Drop       Invited to
in the wild       "The Inner          + Unboxing          Inner Circle
(organic /        Circle"             Ritual              privileges
referral)         registration        + Day-3 note        (see below)
```

---

### THE INNER CIRCLE PRIVILEGE LADDER

Not every Patron is at the same tier. The ladder below is *never explicitly communicated* — it is *felt* through the difference in how each tier is treated.

| Tier | Definition | What They Receive |
|---|---|---|
| **The Waitlist** | Has registered interest, not yet purchased | Access to the purchase link 24 hours before public (if any public release exists) |
| **First Patron** | Has completed one purchase | Personal thank-you note from Rosemary. Named in "Wear Report" if they share. |
| **Returning Patron** | Has completed two or more purchases | Early preview of next collection before waitlist opens. Direct WhatsApp access to Rosemary or senior CM. |
| **House Patron** | Collector of 3+ pieces | Invited to private fittings / preview events. First right of refusal on made-to-order pieces. Named in brand archive. |

---

### THE POST-PURCHASE MESSAGE SEQUENCE

This is the *exact* cadence of personal communication after a purchase. It should feel like messages from a person, not a CRM.

---

---

### THE PATRON WALL

A dedicated Instagram Highlight (or a section on the website, if one exists) curating real Patron styling moments. Rules:

- **Always credit by first name and city.** Never anonymous. Never stock-looking.
- **Curate for diversity of occasion** — not just the most glamorous shots. A Patron wearing The Fold at a Jakarta café on a Tuesday is as valuable as a Kondangan photo.
- **Caption every Patron Wall post in the brand voice.** Example: *"Karin, Jakarta. She wore it to a gallery opening and then to breakfast the next morning. The Fold doesn't clock out. Neither does she."*

---

### THE NAMING RITUAL — "BECOMING A PATRON"

When a customer completes their first purchase, they receive a physical card inside the unboxing (coordinated with the packaging team) with the following text:

> *"From this moment, you are not a customer of Rosemary Legoh.*
> *You are a Patron of The House.*
>
> *A Patron is someone who understood the value of the work before the world did.*
> *We do not forget that.*
>
> *Welcome to the Inner Circle.*
> *— Rosemary Legoh"*

This single card does more for retention than any loyalty points program. It reframes the transaction as *initiation*.

## PART V: COMMUNITY MANAGEMENT OPERATIONAL STANDARDS

---

### RESPONSE TIME COMMITMENTS

| Channel | Response Commitment |
|---|---|
| Instagram DM (Patron inquiry) | Within 4 hours during business hours |
| WhatsApp (Inner Circle members) | Within 2 hours |
| Instagram Comments (on feed posts) | Within 24 hours |
| Story replies | Acknowledge within 24 hours; feature if relevant |

---

### THE "THREE NONS" — WHAT ROSEMARY LEGOH NEVER DOES IN COMMUNITY

1. **Never compares.** We do not mention competitors, trends, or market positioning in community communications. We exist in our own aesthetic universe.
2. **Never discounts publicly.** Price reductions, if they ever occur, happen privately to specific Patrons via direct message — never in a public post or broadcast.
3. **Never over-posts.** A maximum of **one feed post per week** and **three Stories per week** (non-promotional days). Scarcity of content mirrors scarcity of product. Every post must earn its place.

---

### CONTENT CALENDAR SKELETON

| Week | Feed Post | Stories | WhatsApp Broadcast |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Week 1** | Material Diary (carousel) | Artisan hands video + poll | — |
| **Week 2** | Patron Wall feature | Style Challenge prompt + entries | Drop reminder to waitlist |
| **Week 3** | Founder reflection / brand philosophy | Behind-the-scenes production | — |
| **Week 4** | Drop announcement OR editorial image | Countdown (3 Stories across 3 days) | Inner Circle early access link |

---

## CLOSING NOTE: THE PHILOSOPHY OF PATRONAGE

> *The difference between a customer and a Patron is not how much they spend. It is what they believe they are part of.*
>
> *A customer buys a garment. A Patron joins a House.*
>
> *Every communication from Rosemary Legoh — every caption, every DM, every card in every box — must make the recipient feel that they arrived early to something important. That they saw the craft before it was famous. That they are, in some small way, responsible for the story being told.*
>
> *That belief is the most defensible asset in the brand's portfolio. No copycat can replicate it.*
