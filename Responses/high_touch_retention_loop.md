# The "High-Touch" Retention Loop
### *VIP Post-Purchase Experience for Rosemary Legoh*

---

## Strategic Overview

The most underestimated revenue engine in boutique fashion is not the first sale — it is the **second**. A customer who repurchases is not a transaction; she is a convert. This framework transforms a completed purchase into the opening chapter of a long-form relationship, making every follow-up feel less like a marketing message and more like a handwritten letter from the designer herself.

The architecture is built on three psychological pillars:

1. **Belonging** — She did not just buy a garment. She joined something.
2. **Recognition** — She is seen by name, not by order number.
3. **Anticipation** — She is given a reason to return before she has finished wearing what she bought.

---

## THE THREE-MESSAGE SEQUENCE

---

### ✦ MESSAGE 1 — "The Story Behind What You Now Own"
**Timing:** 24–48 hours after the order is delivered (confirmed via waybill tracking).
**Channel:** WhatsApp personal message (from the owner's number, not a business account).
**Tone:** Warm, intimate, and informative. Like receiving a note tucked inside the box — only it arrives in your pocket.

---

**MESSAGE TEMPLATE (Indonesian + English):**

> *Halo [First Name],*
>
> *Rosemary di sini. 🙏*
>
> *I wanted to be the first to reach you after your piece arrived. What you are holding is not just fabric and structure — it carries something older.*
>
> *The outer you received uses [specific Wastra name, e.g., Tenun Lurik from Solo] — a cloth that has been hand-woven in [region] for generations. The weavers who made yours work on narrow looms that haven't changed since the 1930s. Each thread is placed by hand.*
>
> *The RL Fold at the collar? It was designed to honour the 'wiron' — the precise, ritual pleating technique used in Javanese ceremonial dress — but reinterpreted for structure and movement. When you wear it, that history moves with you.*
>
> *Take a moment with it. I hope it feels like it was made for you, because in a very real sense, it was.*
>
> *With warmth,*
> *Rosemary*

---

**Design Notes for This Message:**
- **Never use a template blast.** The customer's first name, the specific Wastra type she received, and her region should be manually inserted. This message should take 3–4 minutes to personalise. That time is the product.
- **No CTA, no link, no discount code.** The moment you ask for something, the intimacy is broken. This message asks for nothing.
- **Optionally attach:** A single high-quality photo — the weavers' hands, or the raw fabric before construction. One image. No carousel.

---

### ✦ MESSAGE 2 — "We'd Love to See How You Wear It"
**Timing:** 10 days after delivery.
**Channel:** WhatsApp, or Instagram DM if the customer is active there.
**Tone:** Community-driven, celebratory, and low-pressure. An invitation, not a request.

---

**MESSAGE TEMPLATE:**

> *[First Name], it has been 10 days — has the piece found its place in your wardrobe?*
>
> *We are building something quietly beautiful on our community wall: a real-life gallery of how women across Indonesia are interpreting the RL Fold in their own way.*
>
> *No professional lighting required. No need to look "editorial." We want the Tuesday morning version — the coffee run, the meeting, the kondangan. Real wear.*
>
> *If you feel like sharing: snap a style selfie and send it here, or tag us privately on Instagram [@rosemarylegohatelier]. You decide if it stays private or if we share it with credit to you.*
>
> *Either way — I'd genuinely love to see it.*
>
> *— Rosemary*

---

**Design Notes for This Message:**
- **"You decide" is the key phrase.** It removes the pressure of public exposure while still encouraging participation. Many customers will share precisely because they feel they have control.
- **Community wall** should be a real, visible Instagram Highlight titled "Worn" or "Our Women" — not just a promise. If it doesn't yet exist, build it before this message rolls out.
- **Social proof loop:** Every selfie received (with permission) populates the wall, which then becomes a conversion asset for future customers. The retention loop generates marketing content.
- **Response protocol:** When a selfie is received, Rosemary (or a trusted team member trained to write in her voice) replies within 2 hours with a personal, specific compliment. e.g.: *"The way the fold sits against that ivory blouse — I didn't even plan that combination, but it's perfect."*

---

### ✦ MESSAGE 3 — "You Hear It First"
**Timing:** 30 days after delivery.
**Channel:** WhatsApp (primary). Email if the customer is less WhatsApp-active.
**Tone:** Exclusive, conspiratorial, and forward-looking. Like being let backstage before the curtain rises.

---

**MESSAGE TEMPLATE:**

> *[First Name],*
>
> *I don't send this to everyone.*
>
> *Next month, we are releasing the next small batch — 30 pieces, a new structure, a new Wastra source I've been testing for almost a year. I am not announcing it publicly yet.*
>
> *But you were one of our first. That means you get to see it before anyone else — and before the waitlist opens.*
>
> *I'll send you the first-look preview in [X] days. No obligation. Just the first eyes.*
>
> *Thank you for trusting us with something you'll wear.*
>
> *Rosemary*

---

**Design Notes for This Message:**
- **"I don't send this to everyone"** is factually true and psychologically powerful. Exclusivity is not a trick — it is a design principle. Limit this message to customers who have previously purchased.
- **The preview itself** (sent a few days later as promised) should be a single behind-the-scenes photo: a sketch, a swatch, or a detail shot — nothing fully revealed. Anticipation requires restraint.
- **The invitation loop:** After the preview is sent, follow up with: *"Would you like to be on the VIP list for early access?"* This is where the waitlist funnel reactivates organically, from a customer base that already trusts the brand.

## THE SYSTEM BEHIND THE SEQUENCE

### CRM Architecture (Simple, Scalable)

At minimum, maintain a Google Sheet with the following columns:

| Column | Purpose |
|---|---|
| Customer Name | First name for personalisation |
| WhatsApp Number | Direct message channel |
| Instagram Handle | Optional DM channel |
| Order Date | Anchor for message timing |
| Wastra Type Purchased | For Message 1 textile personalisation |
| Message 1 Sent (Y/N + Date) | Delivery tracking |
| Message 2 Sent (Y/N + Date) | Follow-up tracking |
| Selfie Received (Y/N) | Community wall pipeline |
| Message 3 Sent (Y/N + Date) | Re-engagement tracking |
| VIP Status (Y/N) | Tier qualification |
| Notes | Any personal details to reference in future messages |

Set calendar reminders for Day 1, Day 10, and Day 30 after each confirmed delivery. This process is manual by design. The premium is in the human touch.

---

### Voice Guidelines: Writing as Rosemary

Every message must pass four filters before sending:

1. **Would Rosemary say this in person?** If it sounds like a newsletter, rewrite it.
2. **Is there anything being asked for?** Remove it from Messages 1 and 2.
3. **Does it reference something specific to this customer?** If it could have been sent to anyone, it should not be sent.
4. **Is it short enough to feel personal?** Long messages signal effort; short messages signal confidence. Aim for under 120 words per message.

---

### Escalation: The Handwritten Note Protocol

For customers who respond warmly to Message 1 or share a selfie in Message 2, consider a physical escalation:

- A small notecard (Rosemary Legoh branded, hand-addressed) slipped into the next order they receive — even if they didn't ask for anything.
- Contents: 2–3 handwritten lines acknowledging something they said or shared. Nothing promotional.
- This costs less than IDR 10,000 to execute and generates the highest referral rate of any touchpoint in the funnel.

---

### Measuring the Loop

Track the following monthly:

| Metric | Target Benchmark |
|---|---|
| Message 1 Open/Read Rate | >95% (WhatsApp read receipts) |
| Message 2 Selfie Submission Rate | >20% of recipients |
| Message 3 to Waitlist Conversion | >40% of recipients |
| Repeat Purchase Rate (90 days) | >30% of VIP-messaged customers |
| Community Wall Posts Published | 3–5 per drop cycle |

## PHILOSOPHICAL PRINCIPLE

The fashion industry has spent decades training customers to chase the next thing. The Rosemary Legoh retention system inverts this logic: **it makes the customer feel so seen after the purchase that she is not thinking about the next brand — she is waiting for the next drop.**

The three messages are not marketing. They are the continuation of a conversation that began when she decided to trust a small-batch designer with her wardrobe. That trust is the most valuable asset this brand has.

Guard it carefully. Honour it consistently. It compounds.
