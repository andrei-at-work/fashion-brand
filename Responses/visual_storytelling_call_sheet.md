## 🎭 CREATIVE BRIEF SUMMARY

The **RL Fold** is not a garment — it is a structural argument. Every shot must communicate this. We are not shooting *fashion*; we are documenting **architecture worn by a human body.** The model is the plinth. The Fold is the sculpture.

The reference world: Zaha Hadid sketches, Vogue Indonesia's 2022 "Wastra Reimagined" issue, Antonie Helene portraiture, and the interior negative spaces of Jakarta's National Library.

**Emotional Register:** Still. Considered. Quietly powerful. Never posed — always *arrived.*

---

## 👤 MODEL ARCHETYPE

### The "Urban Matriarch"

**Physical Profile:**
- Age range: 32–45 years
- Build: Lean-to-medium. Shoulders square and defined — critical for fold geometry to read correctly in frame.
- Height: 165–172 cm preferred (ensures outer proportions fall at intended hem and sleeve points)
- Complexion: Rich warm-toned skin — essential to contrast the linen and structured Wastra fabric tones

**Psychological Profile:**
This model does not perform for the camera. She **occupies space.** Her expression communicates a woman who owns rooms before she speaks. She is not aspirational in the traditional commercial sense — she is *recognizable* to the target consumer. The Rosemary Legoh woman sees herself, not a stranger.

**Casting Direction:**
Avoid commercial-catalogue energy. Look for talent with experience in editorial, fine art, or conceptual work. Test shots should show comfort with stillness and micro-movement. Avoid excessive smiling — warmth should come from posture and presence, not expression.

**Hair & Makeup Brief (MUA Direction):**
- Hair: Sleek, structured, away from the collar. The fold's neckline asymmetry must never be obscured. Options: low chignon, architectural up-do, or natural textured coil held back.
- Makeup: "Skin-first" — minimal base, defined brow, a single tonal lip that reads architectural (dusty rose, terracotta, or near-nude mahogany). No shimmer. No heavy contour — the shadow work is the lighting director's job.
- No accessories at the collar or neckline. Hardware (ring, cuff) only on hands.

## 💡 LIGHTING LANGUAGE

### Primary Directive: "Shadow as Structure"

The **RL Fold** was designed with shadow in mind. The asymmetric planes of the collar and hem create natural ledges that catch directional light. The lighting director's mandate is to **sculpt with shadow**, not illuminate uniformly.

---

### Setup 1 — "The Fold Reveal" (Hero Shot)

| Parameter | Setting |
|---|---|
| **Type** | Single hard source — large beauty dish or 5K Fresnel |
| **Angle** | 45° camera-left, elevated to 60° above horizon |
| **Fill** | Black v-flat opposite side — zero fill. Let shadow fall fully. |
| **Background** | Seamless warm ecru or raw concrete wall |
| **Lens** | 85mm prime f/1.8–f/2.8 |
| **Intent** | The fold's upper asymmetric ridge catches the key light, casting a deep geometric shadow across the chest. This is the money shot — the shadow *draws the fold*. |

---

### Setup 2 — "The Silhouette/Movement Study"

| Parameter | Setting |
|---|---|
| **Type** | Backlit — large diffused source (4x6 scrim or window with direct morning sun) |
| **Angle** | Directly behind talent, slightly elevated |
| **Fill** | Rim light (strip box) from 90° camera-right to define edge of fold at shoulder |
| **Background** | Blown-out white or high-key concrete |
| **Lens** | 85mm or 135mm |
| **Intent** | Full body silhouette shot. The RL Fold's structural outer shape reads as pure line — reminiscent of architectural elevation drawings. |

---

### Setup 3 — "Close Geometry" (Detail + Texture)

| Parameter | Setting |
|---|---|
| **Type** | Macro/close-up. Hard raking light from side — 90° to surface |
| **Angle** | Low, at fabric plane level |
| **Fill** | None — full raking to reveal textile texture and hand-fold precision |
| **Background** | Not visible (tight crop) |
| **Lens** | 100mm macro or 85mm with extension tube |
| **Intent** | Document the *craft* of the fold. Every stitch, every pleat ridge, the weight and drape of the Wastra all read as intentional engineering. |

## 🤸 5 POWER POSES — "The RL Fold Language"

Each pose has a name, a structural intent, a technical direction for the model, and the Instagram crop format it is designed for.

---

### Pose 1 — "The Load-Bearing Wall"
*Static Hero. Full Body.*

**Model Direction:**
- Stand in profile, weight shifted onto the back leg, front foot lightly forward.
- Arms: one hand in trouser pocket (or resting along outer seam), other arm hanging loose.
- Chin: slightly lowered, gaze directed out of frame toward mid-distance. No eye contact with camera.
- The body creates a vertical architectural line. The outer's asymmetric hem and fold collar cantilever off this line.

**Technical Note:** Shoot in portrait orientation. The slight profile angle means the fold's front asymmetry is visible while the back structural seam reads at the shoulder. This is the **hero carousel slide**.

**Format:** Instagram portrait 4:5

---

### Pose 2 — "The Cantilever"
*Dynamic. Three-Quarter View.*

**Model Direction:**
- Model walks at approximately 30° toward camera — a composed, mid-stride capture, NOT a runway walk. This is a considered step, not motion-blurred energy.
- The leading arm swings naturally forward — this causes the outer's structured panel to open and the fold's underside geometry to become visible.
- Head: facing 45° away from camera. The fold collar frames the exposed neck and jaw perfectly.

**Technical Note:** Capture at 1/500 sec or faster to freeze fabric drape at maximum articulation point. The moving outer creates a "suspended architecture" effect.

**Format:** Instagram portrait 4:5 / Lookbook full-spread crop

---

### Pose 3 — "The Overhang"
*Seated or Perched. Architectural Setting.*

**Model Direction:**
- Model seated on a hard-edge surface (concrete ledge, sculptural plinth, deep window sill). Perched, not slouched.
- Both hands rest in lap or on surface edge. Back straight — near military posture.
- The outer's fabric should fall and pool slightly at the seated hem, demonstrating the *weight* and *drape quality* of the Wastra.
- Direct gaze to camera. This is the only pose with eye contact — it communicates ownership of space.

**Technical Note:** Eye-level or slightly below — camera should not look down on the subject. The seated position allows the fold collar to be photographed at face height, making it the visual equal of the model's expression.

**Format:** Square 1:1 (community post) / Instagram portrait 4:5

---

### Pose 4 — "The Negative Space"
*Minimalist. Back to Camera.*

**Model Direction:**
- Back fully to camera. Standing in an open space.
- Arms relaxed at sides, palms facing back.
- Weight centered — absolute stillness.
- The **back panel's structural seam and fold architecture** are the full subject of this image.

**Technical Note:** This is the most architectural shot. The model's back becomes a facade. Lighting Setup 1 should be adapted — key light now rakes across *the back*, reading every structural line of the fold construction where the rear panels meet. Use a very slight rim light to separate the silhouette from background.

**Format:** Instagram portrait 4:5 / Story 9:16

---

### Pose 5 — "The Reveal Hand"
*Detail. Hands + Collar. Close Intimacy.*

**Model Direction:**
- Model turns head to expose one side of the neck and fold collar.
- One hand rises — not styling the collar, but resting fingertips *lightly* at the collarbone, below the fold. The hand frames the asymmetry without touching it.
- Expression: quiet contemplation. Eyes can close or gaze down.

**Technical Note:** This is the *narrative close-up*. Shoot with Setup 3 lighting (raking close light) to capture the fold's tactile pleat structure at this intimate distance. The hand grounds the image in human scale — making the structural fold feel personal, not institutional.

**Format:** Instagram square 1:1 / Carousel second or third slide

## ✅ ON-SET QUALITY REMINDERS

1. **Check the fold before every shot.** Designate one person (stylist or art director) whose sole responsibility is fold placement and collar asymmetry integrity.
2. **The fold's front asymmetric ridge must always be the highest point** — if it collapses or flattens in frame, pause and reset.
3. **No on-set music that creates body-sway energy** — this is a still, architectural shoot. Ambient/minimal sound only.
4. **Limit set presence** — maximum 8 people on set at any time. Quiet luxury starts with quiet production.
5. **Film + Digital:** If budget allows, one roll of medium format film on the Hero Shots (Pose 1 & 2) provides a tonal richness that no digital preset can replicate. Use this for eventual print lookbook.
