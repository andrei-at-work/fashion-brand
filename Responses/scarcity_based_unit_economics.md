## PREAMBLE: THE ECONOMICS OF INTENTION

A 30-unit batch is not a limitation. It is the strategy. In the luxury niche, scarcity is not what happens when you run out — it is what you engineer before you begin. This document gives you the financial architecture to price with confidence, communicate value without apology, and build a margin structure that sustains the level of service your customer expects.

## PART I: THE PRICING FORMULA

### The 65% Gross Margin Mandate

Your target gross margin of **65%** is not arbitrary. It is the minimum threshold for a luxury small-batch brand to fund:
- Premium packaging and unboxing ritual (estimated 8–12% of revenue)
- High-touch post-purchase service (time cost per customer)
- Brand photography and content creation amortised across the drop
- Repair/replacement reserve for any RL Fold structural defect (non-negotiable)
- Reinvestment into the next fabric development cycle

The formula is:

$$\text{Gross Margin \%} = \frac{\text{Selling Price} - \text{COGS}}{\text{Selling Price}} \times 100$$

Rearranged to solve for Selling Price at a target margin:

$$\text{Selling Price} = \frac{\text{COGS}}{1 - \text{Target Margin}}$$

$$\text{Selling Price} = \frac{[C]}{1 - 0.65} = \frac{[C]}{0.35} = [C] \times 2.857$$

**In plain language:** multiply your COGS by **2.857** to find the floor price that achieves exactly 65% gross margin.

> **Prestige Price Rounding Rule:** Never round down to the nearest hundred thousand. Round up to a number that *ends in* 900,000 or 200,000/400,000/800,000. Avoid "round" numbers like Rp 5,000,000 — they feel like they were calculated, not curated. A price of Rp 5,200,000 feels considered.

### The Actual Margin You Should Target: 68–72%

65% is the **floor**, not the goal. Here is why you should price to **68–72% gross margin**:

| Margin | Price on Rp 1,500,000 COGS | What the Extra % Buys |
|---|---|---|
| 65% | Rp 4,400,000 | Baseline operations |
| 68% | Rp 4,700,000 | Absorbs one customs delay or fabric rework |
| 72% | Rp 5,400,000 | Funds a pop-up event OR a paid micro-influencer for next drop |

**Recommendation:** Set your public price at the **70% margin anchor**. You will still close on early-adopter urgency; you will have room to offer a private 5% "Inner Circle" acknowledgment (not discount — a gift-with-purchase or priority sizing access) without eroding your margin floor.

> **Key insight:** Once you account for packaging and logistics, your *effective* net margin on a Rp 5,000,000 price point with Rp 1,500,000 COGS is approximately **63%** — still healthy, and still above your 65% gross margin requirement when packaging is funded separately from production.

---

### The "Sold Out" Revenue Target

If all 30 units sell at Rp 5,000,000:
- **Total Revenue:** Rp 150,000,000
- **Total Net Contribution:** Rp 93,990,000

If you sell 25 of 30 (83% sell-through, a conservative scenario):
- **Total Revenue:** Rp 125,000,000
- **Total Net Contribution:** Rp 67,325,000

**Holding 5 units unsold costs you Rp 26,665,000 in revenue but does NOT result in a loss — your COGS on those 5 units (Rp 7,500,000) is the only sunk cost.** This is the structural advantage of low-volume, high-margin: even partial sell-through is profitable.

The remaining 5 "unsold" units can serve as:
1. Gifted to the top 5 Inner Circle clients as a "Final Piece" private purchase invitation
2. Held for the next trunk show or private styling session
3. Photographed for catalog and archived — never discounted, never moved to clearance

## PART III: COMMUNICATING SMALL BATCH VALUE

This is where most boutique designers lose the sale. The price is right; the story is wrong. Your customer has already decided emotionally — your job is to give her the rational justification she needs to complete the transaction.

---

### The Three Pillars of Small-Batch Justification

**Pillar 1 — Finite Supply as a Feature, Not an Apology**

Never frame scarcity as a limitation. Frame it as curation.

> ❌ Wrong: *"We only make 30 pieces per collection."*
> ✅ Right: *"Each drop is deliberately limited to 30 pieces — the maximum number the lead tailor can produce without compromising the RL Fold's structural integrity."*

The second sentence does three things: it names the designer's process, it establishes a technical threshold, and it makes the customer feel that her piece is the result of a deliberate craft decision, not a capacity constraint.

---

**Pillar 2 — The COGS Story (Without Revealing the Number)**

Your customer does not need to know your COGS. She needs to understand *where the cost lives*. Build a visual cost narrative:

> *"The fabric alone — handwoven ATBM batik from Pekalongan — takes three weeks to weave for a single bolt. The RL Fold collar requires 14 individual construction steps before it can be permanently set. Every unit passes a 15-point quality check and is photographed against a Master Reference before it is approved to ship."*

This is not boasting. It is transparency. It moves the conversation from "why is it so expensive?" to "of course it costs this much."

---

**Pillar 3 — The Investment Framing**

The affluent urban Indonesian woman at your price point does not think of fashion as expense — she thinks of it as portfolio. Position accordingly:

> *"A Rosemary Legoh outer is not seasonal. The RL Fold is not a trend — it is a structural signature that has no reference in the current market. In three years, this piece will be more relevant, not less."*

This is the language of heirlooms. It is also the language of investment. It reframes the purchase from consumption to acquisition.

---

### The "Price Reveal" Communication Framework

Use this sequence when introducing price to a new customer (WhatsApp, DM, or in-person):

1. **Lead with the scarcity anchor:** *"This drop is 30 pieces only."*
2. **Follow with the craft detail:** *"The RL Fold takes our senior tailor 3 days per unit."*
3. **State the price with confidence, no softening:** *"Each piece is Rp 5,200,000."*
4. **Immediately pivot to access, not justification:** *"Priority sizing is reserved for the waitlist. Would you like me to hold your preferred size?"*

> **Rule:** Never apologise for the price. Never volunteer a comparison to mass-market. If a customer says "that's expensive," the correct response is: *"It is. That's by design."*

### Pricing Communication Across Channels

| Channel | Tone | Price Display Strategy |
|---|---|---|
| **Instagram Feed** | Aspirational, no number | Show the fold detail. Never display price in the caption. Price is reserved for direct conversation. |
| **Instagram Stories** | Teaser/Urgency | *"30 pieces. DM for sizing."* Price revealed only via DM. |
| **WhatsApp Broadcast** | Direct, personal | Full price + payment options + size availability. This is where the transaction closes. |

| **In-Person / Trunk Show** | Consultative | Never quote price first. Walk through the construction, then reveal price as a natural conclusion of the story. |

## PART IV: THE PRICE INTEGRITY RULES

These rules protect the brand's perceived value across all future drops.

1. **No public discounts. Ever.** A Rosemary Legoh piece does not go on sale. If a unit remains unsold after 90 days, it is retired to the archive, gifted to a strategic relationship, or held for a private styling event. It is never discounted publicly.

2. **Price increases between drops are expected.** As the brand builds reputation, price each subsequent drop 5–15% higher than the last. Communicate this proactively to the waitlist: *"The next drop will reflect our new fabric partnership with [weaver]. Pricing will open at Rp X."* This creates urgency for current-drop buyers and positions the brand as ascending.

3. **The price is the same for everyone.** No VIP pricing, no "just for you" deals. Your Inner Circle gets *access* privileges (first sizing selection, private previews) — not price privileges. Equal pricing protects brand equity and prevents resentment among your community.

4. **Instalment options are acceptable; discounts are not.** If a customer needs a payment plan, offer a 2-instalment option (50% upon order, 50% before dispatch). The total price does not change. Frame it as a service, not a concession.

5. **Know your walk-away price.** If your actual COGS rises due to fabric costs, labour, or currency shifts, do not absorb the increase into your margin. Raise the price and communicate it as a reflection of the quality standard being maintained.

## CLOSING: THE PRICE IS THE STATEMENT

In the quiet luxury market, the price is not the last thing your customer learns about a piece. It is the first thing she feels. The right price — one that you can hold with composure, explain with specificity, and defend without a discount — signals that you know exactly what you have made, and so does she.

Thirty pieces at Rp 5,200,000 is a Rp 156,000,000 statement. Make it without blinking.
