# ✦ THE WAITLIST DROP STRATEGY ✦
### *Rosemary Legoh — Digital Queue Architecture for High-Demand Scarcity*

---

## STRATEGIC OVERVIEW

**Objective:** Engineer a pre-launch sequence that transforms passive followers into an ordered, anticipatory waitlist — positioning the purchase link as a *privilege*, not a notification.

**Core Tension to Exploit:** Desire vs. Access. Every day of the 7-day sequence increases desire. The purchase link resolves that tension for the chosen few on the waitlist.

**Platform Logic:**
- **Instagram Stories** — Daily tease mechanism (reaches existing audience, passive consumption)
- **Instagram DMs / Link-in-Bio Form** — Waitlist opt-in gate
- **WhatsApp Broadcast List** — The exclusive channel where the purchase link is delivered

---

## THE WAITLIST ARCHITECTURE

### Phase 0: Pre-Sequence Setup (3–5 Days Before Day 1)
Before the 7-day sequence begins, build the infrastructure:

1. **Create a WhatsApp Broadcast List** — "Rosemary Legoh Inner Circle Drop 〇〇" (use drop number, not date)
2. **Set up a simple Google Form or Typeform** as the waitlist gate. Collect:
   - Name
   - WhatsApp number
   - Size (S/M/L/XL) — *this is a psychological commitment device; asking size signals purchase intent*
   - "Why do you want this piece?" (optional — adds ritual, strengthens emotional investment)
3. **Pin Stories Highlight** titled "THE FOLD" to your profile — leave it empty until Day 1
4. **Set a DM auto-reply** (using Meta Business Suite): *"The inner circle for our next drop is forming. Be first: [link to form]"*

## THE 7-DAY INSTAGRAM STORIES CALENDAR

### ✦ DAY 1 — THE ABSENCE
**Concept:** Say nothing. Show nothing finished. Create a vacuum.

- **Story 1 (Morning):** Close-up of a workspace — fabric bolts, chalk marks, tailor's tools. No text. No music. Black and white filter.
- **Story 2 (Afternoon):** A single line of text on a dark background:
  > *"Something is being built."*
- **Story 3 (Evening):** Your hands (or the tailor's hands) holding a single piece of uncut Wastra fabric. No caption.

**Action:** Add all three Stories to the "THE FOLD" Highlight.
**CTA:** None. Restraint is the message.

---

### ✦ DAY 2 — THE MATERIAL
**Concept:** Introduce the fabric. Make the material feel rare before the garment is seen.

- **Story 1 (Morning):** Extreme close-up of the Wastra weave — macro lens or phone zoomed in. Text overlay: *"Sourced. Not mass-ordered."*
- **Story 2 (Afternoon):** A 6-second Boomerang of fabric being pulled taut, testing drape and rigidity. No music — let the fabric sound.
- **Story 3 (Evening):** Static card: Motif name, origin region (e.g., *Solo*), and weaver's collective name. Clean sans-serif typography on cream background.

**Action:** Add to Highlight.
**CTA:** None still. Let curiosity compound.

---

### ✦ DAY 3 — THE QUESTION
**Concept:** Introduce the idea of *The RL Fold* without showing it. Pose a question that demands engagement.

- **Story 1 (Morning):** A text card:
  > *"There is a fold at the collar that can't be pressed flat. It's supposed to be that way."*
- **Story 2 (Midday):** Poll sticker — *"Do you know what makes a collar structural vs. decorative?"* Options: **Yes / I want to know**
- **Story 3 (Evening):** Short text response to the poll: *"We'll show you in 4 days."*

**Action:** Add to Highlight.
**First CTA Introduced:** Add a Link sticker on Story 3 — *"Join the waitlist before it opens to the public."* Direct to your Google Form.

---

### ✦ DAY 4 — THE HAND
**Concept:** Show the human skill. Humanize the construction without fully revealing the outcome.

- **Story 1 (Morning):** A 15-second video of the tailor's hands folding and aligning the collar — filmed from above, natural light only. No voiceover. Play ambient workshop audio.
- **Story 2 (Afternoon):** Text card:
  > *"The RL Fold requires 11 independent alignment points. Each one is done by hand."*
- **Story 3 (Evening):** Side-by-side: a mass-market collar vs. your structured collar (show from behind, no brand visible on competitor piece). Text: *"The difference is the intention."*

**Action:** Add to Highlight.
**CTA (Urgency Layer 1):** Story 2 Link sticker — *"Waitlist closes in 3 days. 30 units only."*

---

### ✦ DAY 5 — THE SILHOUETTE
**Concept:** First glimpse of the completed garment — but only in silhouette. The full reveal is gated behind the waitlist.

- **Story 1 (Morning):** A shadowed, backlit photo of a model wearing the piece. The silhouette is clear; the details are not. Text: *"This is The RL Fold."*
- **Story 2 (Afternoon):** Slow 10-second video panning from collar to hem. High contrast. No face shown. Music: minimal, architectural — something like Brian Eno or a Garageband ambient loop.
- **Story 3 (Evening):** Countdown sticker — set to the moment the purchase link goes live (48 hours out).
  Text: *"The link goes to the waitlist first. Not the feed."*

**Action:** Add to Highlight.
**CTA (Urgency Layer 2):** Link sticker on every story today — *"You have 48 hours to register."*

---

### ✦ DAY 6 — THE SOCIAL PROOF & FINAL CALL
**Concept:** Create the feeling that others are moving. Waitlist psychology depends on visible demand.

- **Story 1 (Morning):** Screenshot (anonymized) of a waitlist confirmation message or DM response — e.g., *"I've been waiting for this piece since your last drop."* Overlay your response: *"She's on the list."*
- **Story 2 (Midday):** A simple stat card: *"126 women have registered. 30 units exist."* (Use your real number — even if it's 40, the scarcity math works)
- **Story 3 (Evening):** Final call. Full-screen text:
  > *"Waitlist closes tonight at 11:59 PM.*
  > *The purchase link will be sent tomorrow.*
  > *If you're not on the list — you're not in the queue."*

**Action:** Add to Highlight.
**CTA (Final):** Link sticker on every Story. Also update bio link to the waitlist form for the final 24 hours.

---

### ✦ DAY 7 — THE RELEASE
**Concept:** The day of fulfillment. Reward the waitlist. Make the public feel they missed something real.

**Morning — The Internal Send:**
- At 9:00 AM, send the purchase link exclusively via WhatsApp Broadcast List
- Message template:
  > *"Good morning, [Name]. You are in the Inner Circle.*
  > *The RL Fold — [Drop Name] — is now open for you.*
  > *30 units. Your access closes at 6:00 PM.*
  > [Purchase Link]*
  > *— Rosemary"*
  *(Sign with your name. Not "The Team." You.)*

**Instagram Stories — Public Witness:**
- **Story 1 (9:05 AM):** *"The link has been sent. If you're on the waitlist — check WhatsApp now."*
- **Story 2 (12:00 PM):** A "sold" counter update: *"11 units have found their homes in the last 3 hours."*
- **Story 3 (3:00 PM):** *"7 units remain. Waitlist has first access until 6 PM. Public link opens after."*
- **Story 4 (6:00 PM):** *"Waitlist window closed. [X] units remain. Opening to public now."* *(If sold out: skip this. Post "Sold Out" card instead.)*
- **Story 5 (9:00 PM):** The closure post — sold out or final count. Thank the community. Tease the next fold.

## THE DIGITAL QUEUE MECHANICS

| Mechanism | Tool | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| Waitlist Form | Google Form / Typeform | Captures name, size, WhatsApp — commitment device |
| Purchase Link Delivery | WhatsApp Broadcast | Exclusivity — the feed never gets it first |
| Public Scarcity Signal | Instagram Stories | Creates FOMO in non-waitlist audience |
| Size Pre-registration | Form field | Pre-sells intent, reduces cart abandonment |
| Countdown Timer | Stories Countdown Sticker | Anchors urgency to a specific moment |

---

## POST-DROP: FEEDING THE NEXT WAITLIST

The moment the drop closes — sold out or not — begin the next cycle:

1. **Post a "Missed it?" Story:** *"Subscribe to be first for Drop [N+1]."* Link to a new form.
2. **DM every buyer:** Personal thank-you from Rosemary. Build the VIP tier.
3. **Archive the "THE FOLD" Highlight** with a final "SOLD OUT" card at the top — social proof forever visible on your profile.
4. **Note the waitlist-to-buyer conversion rate.** If it's above 40%, your waitlist is qualified. If below, the Stories sequence needs higher desire-building (usually Days 1–3 need strengthening).

## KEY PSYCHOLOGICAL PRINCIPLES AT WORK

| Principle | Where Applied |
|---|---|
| **Scarcity** | "30 units only" stated repeatedly from Day 4 onward |
| **Social Proof** | Day 6 waitlist count and Day 7 sold counter |
| **Anticipation Architecture** | 7-day slow reveal delays gratification, amplifies desire |
| **Exclusivity Gate** | Purchase link *never* posted publicly on Day 7's first window |
| **Commitment & Consistency** | Size field in waitlist form creates psychological pre-purchase |
| **Authority** | Tailor's craft, 11-point alignment, sourcing story builds premium perception |
| **Personal Connection** | WhatsApp message signed "Rosemary" — not a brand, a person |
