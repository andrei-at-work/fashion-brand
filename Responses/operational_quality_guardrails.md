# ROSEMARY LEGOH: OPERATIONAL QUALITY GUARDRAILS
### *15-Point Quality Control Checklist for The RL Fold Construction*
#### *Authored from the perspective of an Operations Manager, Luxury Garment House*

---

## PREAMBLE

The **RL Fold** is not a decorative element — it is the structural signature of Rosemary Legoh. Its asymmetric origami collar geometry must perform identically across every unit in a batch. One collapsed fold, one uneven pleat, one fraying edge at the collar apex is not a minor defect. It is a brand failure. This document establishes the non-negotiable production standard.

Any unit that fails a checkpoint below is **quarantined** — not discounted, not sold as a second. Quarantined.

---

## PART I: THE 15-POINT QC MASTER CHECKLIST

Each checklist point is assigned a **Gate**: Pre-Cut (PC), In-Construction (IC), or Final Inspection (FI). A unit may only advance to the next production stage after all preceding gates are cleared.

---

### GATE 1 — PRE-CUT (PC): Fabric Integrity

**✦ Point 1 — Pre-Wash Shrinkage Test**
- Cut a 30cm × 30cm swatch from each fabric lot.
- Machine wash on the garment's care setting, air dry flat.
- Re-measure. **Pass standard:** ≤ 3% shrinkage in warp and weft.
- **Fail action:** Reject the lot. Source a replacement. Do not adjust the pattern to compensate for unstable fabric — the fold geometry cannot tolerate variable shrinkage.
- *Document:* Record fabric lot number, supplier, and shrinkage % for every batch.

**✦ Point 2 — Drape & Rigidity Assessment**
- The RL Fold requires a fabric with *controlled body* — enough stiffness to hold the asymmetric pleat geometry without interfering fusibles, yet enough fluidity to fall in a clean line below the collar.
- **Test:** Drape a 50cm × 50cm piece over a curved form (a dress form shoulder is ideal). Observe whether the fabric holds a gentle crease without excessive fraying or collapsing.
- **Pass standard:** Fabric maintains a visible edge crease under its own weight for ≥ 10 seconds.
- *Approved fabrics:* Handwoven batik (double-weave weight), ATBM sarong-grade lurik, composite Tenun ATBM ≥ 120 GSM.

**✦ Point 3 — Colorfastness & Dye Lot Consistency**
- Rub a dampened white cotton cloth against the fabric for 10 strokes (wet crocking test).
- **Pass standard:** No visible dye transfer on the white cloth (Grade 4–5 on the Grey Scale).
- Check all fabric rolls against the approved Batch Reference Swatch. **No visible color deviation** under standard D65 daylight lighting.
- *Document:* Photograph the reference swatch and store it with the production batch file.

**✦ Point 4 — Pattern Template Verification (Master Block)**
- Before cutting any unit, the Fold Master Template must be verified against the approved Master Block on file.
- Lay the production template over the Master Block. **Pass standard:** Zero deviation across all 12 fold reference points.
- Any new pattern grading (see Point 5) must be approved by the lead designer before cutting begins.

**✦ Point 5 — Pattern Grading for Oversized Fit Integrity**
- The RL Fold is engineered on a size M base block. Grading to S, L, and XL must preserve the **fold angle ratio**, not simply scale the collar perimeter.
- **Grading Rule:** The fold apex angle (the defining asymmetric point) is fixed at **42°±2°** across all sizes. Only the collar-band width and shoulder extension are scaled proportionally.
- **Pass standard:** Grade QC officer physically folds and pins a paper mock-up of each graded size before fabric is cut, confirming the apex angle with a protractor.
- *This step is mandatory for every new drop. Do not skip for repeat sizes.*

---

### GATE 2 — IN-CONSTRUCTION (IC): The Fold Execution

**✦ Point 6 — Interfacing Placement Accuracy**
- Fusible interfacing is applied to the fold's inner layer only, cut on the bias to prevent rigid breaklines.
- **Pass standard:** Interfacing edges sit ≥ 5mm inside all seam allowances with no bubbling after pressing at the correct temperature (test on a swatch first per fabric type).
- Interfacing must not cross the fold crease line — this would prevent the pleat from folding cleanly.

**✦ Point 7 — The Fold Construction Sequence**
The RL Fold must be constructed in the following sequence — no deviation is permitted:
1. Baste the pre-fold crease with a long stitch before any permanent sewing.
2. Press the basted fold at ≥ 160°C (per fabric tolerance test) with a damp pressing cloth.
3. Inspect the fold geometry against the Master Template before sewing permanently.
4. Sew the fold closed using a single, continuous lockstitch. No joining mid-seam.
5. Final press to set the fold permanently.
- **Pass standard:** At step 3, the physical fold matches the Master Template within 2mm across all reference points. Any deviation ≥ 3mm requires the fold to be un-basted and restarted.

**✦ Point 8 — Stitch Quality & Tension**
- Stitch length: **2.2mm** for structural seams on the fold; **1.8mm** for topstitching detail.
- Tension: Test on a folded swatch before constructing each unit. No puckering, no thread looping on either face of the fabric.
- **Pass standard:** Pull the structural fold seam with moderate hand pressure. No thread breakage, no seam gaping.
- Thread colour must match or be intentionally contrasted (per the design spec) — no accidental mismatches.

**✦ Point 9 — Asymmetry Verification (The Signature Check)**
- The RL Fold's defining characteristic is its deliberate asymmetry: the right collar lapel extends **3.5cm further** than the left.
- **Pass standard:** After fold construction, lay the garment flat on a cutting table. Measure both collar extensions from the centre-front seam. The differential must be **3.5cm ± 0.3cm**.
- This is the single most critical QC measurement. Record the actual measurement for every unit in the batch log.

**✦ Point 10 — Shoulder & Armscye Fit Check (On-Form Inspection)**
- Every unit is placed on a dress form at the appropriate size before final seaming.
- **Pass standard:** The fold sits flat against the clavicle line with no tension pulling from the shoulder seam. The collar apex points forward, not upward. The body of the outer hangs perpendicular to the floor below the fold.
- Any distortion in the fold caused by incorrect shoulder seam placement requires the shoulder seam to be re-opened and reset.

---

### GATE 3 — FINAL INSPECTION (FI): Finish-Work & Presentation Standard

**✦ Point 11 — Edge Finishing & Internal Seam Quality**
- All internal seams are finished with a Hong Kong (bound) seam using a contrasting or tonal silk tape — this is a detail visible to the customer when the garment is examined closely.
- **Pass standard:** No raw edges visible. No overlocker thread trails. The silk tape lies flat with no twisting.
- This finish communicates *craftsmanship to the connoisseur* — your target customer will check this.

**✦ Point 12 — Button, Hook & Closure Integrity**
- Every closure is tested with 10 open/close cycles before final approval.
- **Pass standard:** No thread pull, no loosening of shank attachment. Button placement must be symmetrical to the garment's centre-front line (± 1mm).
- For press-stud closures used inside the fold: the stud must sit completely hidden when the fold is in its natural closed position.

**✦ Point 13 — Hem Line & Length Consistency**
- **Pass standard:** Hem height is consistent within **± 3mm** around the full circumference of the outer.
- Hem is blind-stitched or hand-fell stitched for boutique retail standard — no machine topstitched hem on finished product.
- Press the hem flat; no rolling or rippling on any section.

**✦ Point 14 — Label Placement & Brand Asset Attachment**
- The woven care label and brand label are placed at the centre-back neck seam — never inside the fold where they would interfere with the drape.
- **Pass standard:** Labels sit flat, with no puckering of the neck seam caused by label thickness. The brand name reads correctly at full hang.
- The hang-tag (with The RL Fold story card) is attached at the left side seam tag loop — not through the garment fabric.

**✦ Point 15 — Final Presentation & Quarantine Decision**
- Every finished unit is steamed (not ironed) by the QC officer, then photographed against a neutral background alongside the Master Reference Unit.
- **Pass standard:** Under direct comparison, there is no visible deviation in fold geometry, collar extension asymmetry, or hem line.
- The unit is then tagged: **APPROVED / QUARANTINE**.
- The QC officer signs the batch log with the unit number. No unit ships without a signed approval.
- **TARGET:** 100% approval rate. Investigate all quarantine units to identify root cause and prevent recurrence in the next batch.

## PART II: PRODUCTION TIMELINE — 30-UNIT BATCH

*This timeline assumes fabric is sourced and approved prior to Day 1. Lead time for fabric procurement from Solo/Pekalongan is handled separately under the Fabric Standards Manual.*

---

| **Phase** | **Days** | **Activity** | **Responsible** |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Pre-Production** | Day 1–2 | Fabric receiving, shrinkage tests (Points 1–3), lot documentation | QC Officer |
| **Pattern Prep** | Day 3 | Template verification, grading approval for all sizes (Points 4–5) | Lead Cutter + Designer |
| **Cutting** | Day 4–5 | All 30 units cut and bundled by size. Interfacing cut simultaneously. | Head Cutter |
| **Interfacing & Basting** | Day 6–7 | Points 6–7, Steps 1–2: Interfacing fused, fold basted and pressed across all units | Senior Tailor |
| **Fold Inspection (Batch)** | Day 8 | Point 7, Step 3: All 30 basted folds inspected against Master Template before any permanent sewing. Failures re-basted same day. | QC Officer + Designer |
| **Fold Construction** | Day 9–11 | Points 7–8: Permanent fold seaming and topstitching. Maximum 10 units per tailor day. | Senior Tailor (×1–2) |
| **Asymmetry Check** | Day 12 | Point 9: Asymmetry measurement logged for all 30 units. Any failing units flagged for correction. | QC Officer |
| **Assembly** | Day 13–15 | Shoulder seaming, side seams, armscye set. On-form inspection (Point 10) for all units. | Tailors |
| **Finishing** | Day 16–18 | Points 11–13: Hong Kong seam binding, closures, hem work. | Finishing Tailors |
| **Labels & Assets** | Day 19 | Point 14: Label and hang-tag attachment across all units. | Finishing Tailors |
| **Final QC** | Day 20–21 | Point 15: Steam, photograph, Master Reference comparison. Batch log signed. Quarantine decisions made. | QC Officer |
| **Buffer & Corrections** | Day 22–23 | Rework of any quarantined units. Re-inspection. | Senior Tailor + QC |
| **Packaging** | Day 24–25 | Boxing, tissue wrapping, heritage card insertion. | Packaging team |
| **Dispatch Ready** | Day 26 | Final unit count confirmed. Batch closed. Approved for release to waitlist. | Operations Manager |

**Total Lead Time: 26 Working Days** (from fabric receipt to dispatch-ready)

> **Note on Scale:** This timeline is calibrated for a single senior tailor working on the RL Fold construction (the most technically demanding phase). Adding a second qualified tailor on Days 9–11 reduces production time by 2–3 days but requires a parallel QC inspection on Day 12. Do not accelerate by skipping the batch fold inspection on Day 8 — this is the highest-value checkpoint in the entire process.
