# ✦ LOGO SUITE — ROSEMARY LEGOH ✦
### *Creative Director's Identity System*
#### *Aesthetic Mandate: Modernist Architecture × Abstract Indonesian Wastra*

---

> *"The finest logos are not designed. They are uncovered — the way a sculptor removes stone to reveal the form that was always waiting inside."*

---

## PRE-DESIGN MANIFESTO

Two visual languages must hold each other in productive tension throughout this identity system. Neither dominates. Neither disappears.

| Modernist Architecture | Indonesian Wastra |
|---|---|
| Load-bearing geometry — the column, beam, threshold | Sacred geometry — kawung circles, parang diagonals, tumpal triangles |
| Swiss precision, negative space as structural logic | Hand-drawn rhythm, the human irregularity in a woven edge |
| Bauhaus reduction: form follows function | Wastra density: pattern carries cosmological meaning |
| The **grid** as an act of control | The **loom** as an act of devotion |
| **Silence between elements** | **Story within every thread** |

The mandate: find the single line where a Mies van der Rohe floor plan and a Javanese *kain panjang* occupy the same visual breath.

---

## DELIVERABLES PER CONCEPT

Each of the three concepts below contains:
1. **Primary Logotype** — structured custom typography specification
2. **Abstract Monogram (The Mark)** — for hardware, tags, and embossing
3. **'Tanah & Laut' Color Palette** — minimalist palette with application rules
4. **Architectural Signature** — the single design idea that makes the concept inevitable

## CONCEPT 01
# "THE THRESHOLD"
### *Architectural Signature: The Portal*

---

### 1) PRIMARY LOGOTYPE

```
┌──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│                                                                  │
│                                                                  │
│   │  R O S E M A R Y  L E G O H  │                              │
│   │                               │                              │
│                                                                  │
│                                                                  │
└──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

Typography Specification:

• "ROSEMARY LEGOH" — Set as a single horizontal line, all caps
  Font class: Geometric sans-serif, custom-drawn
  Tracking: +320 (ultra wide)
  Weight: Hairline (10–15pt stroke)
  The letterforms are reduced to their skeletal geometry —
  each character is built from only straight lines and
  perfect quarter-circles. No humanist curves.

• The two vertical bars flanking the name (│ ... │)
  are drawn at 2pt — noticeably heavier than the letterforms.
  Height: exactly equal to the cap-height of the typography.
  These are the PORTAL PILLARS.

• Spatial Rule: The interior white space between the pillars
  and the first/last letter is exactly 1 cap-height wide.
  This negative space IS the design — it is the threshold.
```

**Architectural Signature — "The Portal":**
In Modernist Architecture, the threshold — the precise moment of transition from outside to inside — is treated as sacred space. Mies van der Rohe designed entire buildings around the approach to a door. Here, the two vertical pillars flank the name not as decoration but as *architectural framing*: they announce that crossing this name is an act of entry. The name itself becomes a doorway. The ultra-wide tracking ensures the letterforms read as structural elements spaced along a load-bearing beam, not as casual lettering.

**Wastra Reference:**
In Javanese *batik pagi-sore* (morning-evening cloth), the boundary line between two pattern fields is called the *seret* — a strict vertical or diagonal division that is never blurred. The two portal pillars in this logotype are the *seret*: the line that defines where sacred space begins. They do not decorate the name; they *consecrate* it.

### 2) THE ABSTRACT MONOGRAM — "THE MARK"

```
        ┌────────────────────────────────┐
        │                                │
        │                                │
        │        │       │               │
        │        │       │               │
        │        └───────┘               │
        │            │                   │
        │            │                   │
        │                                │
        └────────────────────────────────┘

Construction Logic:
• Two vertical strokes of equal height (representing R + L)
  — drawn at 1.5pt, spaced exactly 1 stroke-width apart

• A horizontal connector bridges their bases, forming
  a perfect square bracket: └───────┘

• A single vertical stroke descends from the midpoint
  of the connector, extending downward by 50% of the
  original stroke height

• The result reads simultaneously as:
  — The letter 'π' (pi) — mathematical universality
  — A loom heddle (the mechanism that separates warp threads)
  — The plan view of a threshold: two walls, a floor, a passage
  — The initials R and L sharing a common foundation
```

**Mark Rationale:**
This monogram is designed to function as a *structural diagram* — something an architect might sketch to explain a load path. At 5mm on a sewn tag, it reads as pure geometry. At 30mm on hardware, the careful spacing reveals its dual reading: architectural threshold AND loom heddle. The mark contains no superfluous curves. It is honest. It is built, not drawn.

## CONCEPT 02
# "THE LOOM COLUMN"
### *Architectural Signature: The Colonnade*

---

### 1) PRIMARY LOGOTYPE

```
┌──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│                                                                  │
│                                                                  │
│   R                                                              │
│   O   L                                                          │
│   S   E                                                          │
│   E   G                                                          │
│   M   O                                                          │
│   A   H                                                          │
│   R                                                              │
│   Y                                                              │
│                                                                  │
│                                                                  │
└──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

Typography Specification:

• "ROSEMARY" — Set vertically, reading top-to-bottom
  Font class: High-contrast serif (Didone-inspired, custom)
  Each letter stacked at exact cap-height intervals
  No additional letter-spacing. Letters sit flush, column-tight.
  Weight: Regular (main stroke ~2pt at standard scale)

• "LEGOH" — Set vertically alongside "ROSEMARY"
  Same typeface, but set at 1.5× the scale of ROSEMARY
  Column begins at the baseline of "O" in ROSEMARY —
  creating a deliberate misalignment: LEGOH starts mid-column

• Spatial Rule: The two vertical columns are separated by
  exactly 2 em-widths of the LEGOH scale. This gap is
  an active negative space — the "nave" between columns.

• Horizontal datum line at base: 0.5pt hairline,
  spanning both columns only. Not full-width.
```

**Architectural Signature — "The Colonnade":**
The colonnade is one of architecture's oldest structural poems — columns in sequence create rhythm, procession, and the experience of *arrival*. Set vertically, the two name-columns function as a colonnade viewed in plan: ROSEMARY is the smaller anterior column, LEGOH the larger posterior column, and the white space between them is the covered walkway — the *portico* — where the customer pauses before entering. The misaligned starting points are not a mistake: they represent the structural reality that columns in a colonnade often carry different loads, and that difference is what makes the whole system work.

**Wastra Reference:**
This vertical typographic composition directly mirrors the structure of *tenun ikat* weaving — where warp threads run vertically in taut parallel columns, and the pattern is *locked into* the thread before a single weft passes through. "ROSEMARY" and "LEGOH" are the two dominant warp threads. Everything worn over them takes their form.

### 2) THE ABSTRACT MONOGRAM — "THE MARK"

```
        ┌────────────────────────────────┐
        │                                │
        │                                │
        │          ╷  ╷                  │
        │          │  │                  │
        │          │  │                  │
        │          │  │                  │
        │         ─┴──┴─                 │
        │                                │
        │                                │
        └────────────────────────────────┘

Construction Logic:
• Two vertical strokes of unequal height
  Left stroke: full height (representing 'R')
  Right stroke: 60% height (representing 'L')
  — drawn at 1pt, spaced precisely 1.5pt apart

• A flat horizontal base (2pt weight) caps both strokes
  from below — like a column capital in reverse (a base plate)

• The strokes do not touch the top boundary of their space.
  They hang from the upper field, grounded at the base.
  This is a structural tension: *suspended load*.

• At small scale: reads as a clean geometric glyph
• At large scale: reveals the 'RL' column duality
```

**Mark Rationale:**
The two-column mark is the most *structurally honest* monogram possible for a brand where architecture is the primary metaphor. It does not try to be clever with letterform merging. Instead, it presents two vertical elements of different heights, held together at the base — which is precisely how a colonnade, a loom, and a partnership function. The height differential between the two strokes (left taller, right shorter) echoes the asymmetry of the primary logotype, creating a system-wide visual consistency.

## CONCEPT 03
# "THE PARANG GRID"
### *Architectural Signature: The Structural Bay*

---

### 1) PRIMARY LOGOTYPE

```
┌──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│                                                                  │
│                                                                  │
│        ┌───────────────┐                                         │
│        │ R O S E M A R Y│                                        │
│        └───────────────┘                                         │
│         L  E  G  O  H                                            │
│                                                                  │
│                                                                  │
└──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

Typography Specification:

• "ROSEMARY" — Set inside a precise rectangular enclosure
  (a drawn box, hairline, 0.25pt)
  Font class: Geometric sans-serif, condensed
  All caps, tracking: +80 (moderate)
  The box dimensions are determined entirely by the type —
  it has exactly 0.5 cap-height of padding on all four sides.
  No more, no less.

• "LEGOH" — Set BELOW the box, flush-left with its left edge
  Font class: Same geometric sans, but EXPANDED width (1.6×)
  Weight: Thin (8–10pt stroke at standard scale)
  Tracking: +240 (wide)
  The expanded, wide-tracked LEGOH bleeds past the right edge
  of the box above — creating an intentional overflow.

• Critical Relationship: "LEGOH" is wider than the box.
  The box contains "ROSEMARY" — LEGOH escapes containment.
  This is the core tension of the composition.
```

**Architectural Signature — "The Structural Bay":**
In architecture, a *structural bay* is the fundamental repeating unit of a building's skeleton — a rectangular module defined by four load points (columns). Floors are organized in bays; facades are composed of bay rhythms. Here, the enclosing box around "ROSEMARY" is the bay: a precise, load-bearing module. "LEGOH," wider and uncontained below, is the element that *extends beyond the bay grid* — it is the facade element that pushes past the structural frame, as in the overhanging canopies of Tadao Ando or the protruding volumes of Zaha Hadid's early work. The design says: there is a system here, AND something that exceeds it.

**Wastra Reference:**
The *parang* motif family in Javanese batik is built on a rigorous diagonal grid — a system of interlocking blade forms that tile perfectly across the cloth. But the *parang rusak* (broken parang) deliberately disrupts the grid: one diagonal breaks the pattern, and that break is considered the most powerful and royal variant. "LEGOH" overflowing the box is the *parang rusak* move: the intentional break in the grid that carries the most meaning.

### 2) THE ABSTRACT MONOGRAM — "THE MARK"

```
        ┌────────────────────────────────┐
        │                                │
        │                                │
        │        ┌───┐                   │
        │        │   │                   │
        │        │   └──                 │
        │        │                       │
        │        └──────                 │
        │                                │
        │                                │
        └────────────────────────────────┘

Construction Logic:
• A rectangle, drawn at 1pt, open at two points:
  Top-right corner: open gap = 30% of right side height
  Bottom-right corner: extending horizontal stroke

• The rectangle is NOT closed. It is a contained form
  that deliberately leaks at two calculated points.

• Interior: completely empty. The white space inside is
  the mark's content — a framed void.

• The extending base stroke is exactly as long as the
  rectangle is wide. It creates a floor plane below the frame.

• Reading: The open rectangle simultaneously suggests
  — a structural bay (architecture)
  — the letter 'R' in skeleton form
  — a loom frame with the warp not yet threaded
  — a room that has not yet been entered
```

**Mark Rationale:**
This is the most conceptually ambitious of the three marks. It presents *containment with intentional escape* — a frame that does not fully close. In Indonesian textile tradition, certain sacred cloths are intentionally left with an unfinished edge as a philosophic acknowledgment that only God completes things; the *tumpal* (sacred stitch) is withheld. The open-corner monogram embodies this: it is structurally precise in three corners and deliberately incomplete in the fourth. The brand that knows when *not* to finish is the brand that has mastered its craft.

## SYSTEM SUMMARY

### Concept Decision Matrix

| | CONCEPT 01: THE THRESHOLD | CONCEPT 02: THE LOOM COLUMN | CONCEPT 03: THE PARANG GRID |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Best For** | Brand launch, immediate luxury perception | Editorial campaigns, brand maturity | Contemporary market, design-literate consumer |
| **Architectural Reference** | Portal / Threshold — Mies van der Rohe | Colonnade / Warp Column — Kenzo Tange | Structural Bay / Grid Break — Tadao Ando |
| **Wastra Reference** | *Seret* boundary line, *batik pagi-sore* | *Tenun ikat* warp threads | *Parang rusak* grid disruption |
| **Typography Mood** | Horizontal authority, ultra-wide | Vertical devotion, stacked presence | Contained tension, box-and-overflow |
| **Monogram Mood** | Loom heddle / threshold passage | Twin columns / unequal load | Open frame / intentional incompleteness |
| **Color Temperature** | Warm neutrals (limestone + charcoal) | Earth reds + mangrove greens | Mineral whites + ink blacks |
| **Recommended Application** | Full identity system, retail signage | Lookbook, garment label, tag | Packaging system, e-commerce, social |

### Universal Application Rules

1. **The monogram (The Mark) is never used in color.** It is always rendered in the primary dark tone of whichever palette concept is active, against the primary light ground. No exceptions.
2. **The logotype and The Mark are never placed together at the same visual weight.** On any surface, one leads and the other supports.
3. **The Tanah & Laut palette does not expand.** New colorways for capsule collections are expressed through fabric and garment — never by adding colors to the identity system.
4. **The accent color (bronze, gold, or silver depending on concept) appears only on physical touchpoints:** hardware, emboss, foil stamp, wax seal. It does not appear on digital executions.
