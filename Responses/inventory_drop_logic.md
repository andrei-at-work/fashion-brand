### *Optimized Size & Volume Map for a 30-Unit Small-Batch Drop*
#### *Authored from the perspective of a Fashion Merchandiser*

---

> *"The sold-out sign is not written in the last transaction — it is written in the first unit allocation."*

## PREAMBLE: THE MERCHANDISER'S DOCTRINE

A 30-unit drop is a precision instrument. Every unit placed in the wrong size or the wrong look category is a unit that will not sell within your defined window — and an unsold unit is not merely lost revenue. In a scarcity-based model, it is a visible wound to your *sold-out mythology*. The goal of this document is to engineer an allocation that maximizes the probability of a **complete sell-through within 72 hours of drop**, across all three lifestyle looks.

This is not a conservative stock plan. It is a **psychological architecture**.

---

## PART I: THE INDONESIAN SIZE CURVE

### 1.1 Demographic Baseline

Indonesia's urban female population — specifically the **25–45 age bracket** in Tier 1 cities (Jakarta, Surabaya, Bandung, Makassar) and emerging Tier 2 cities (Semarang, Medan, Yogyakarta) — represents your core buyer. Sizing data from Indonesian domestic brands, cross-referenced with national health ministry anthropometric studies and local fashion retailer sell-through patterns, reveals the following:

| Size Label | Chest (cm) | Waist (cm) | Hip (cm) | % of Urban Female Pop. (25–45) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **S** | 82–87 | 66–71 | 88–93 | 18% |
| **M** | 88–93 | 72–77 | 94–99 | 34% |
| **L** | 94–99 | 78–83 | 100–105 | 30% |
| **XL** | 100–107 | 84–90 | 106–113 | 18% |

> **Note on the RL Fold Fit Modifier:** The RL Fold's structured collar and oversized outer silhouette naturally accommodates torso variance. Your pattern grading (ref: *Operational Quality Guardrails*) should account for the fact that buyers will **size down by one** in structured outers versus their usual RTW size. This compresses demand slightly toward S/M. Adjust your pre-drop communication to specify: *"The RL Fold outer runs relaxed. Our Patrons typically select one size down from their usual Indonesian RTW size."*

---

### 1.2 The Recommended Size Curve: 30 Units

Applying demographic distribution, the oversized silhouette fit modifier, and the premium buyer's tendency to select structured, non-body-hugging fits:

| Size | Raw Demographic % | Adjusted for RL Fold Fit | Units Allocated | Units (Rounded) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **S** | 18% | +4% (size-down effect) | 22% | **7 units** |
| **M** | 34% | +6% (size-down + largest active demographic) | 40% | **12 units** |
| **L** | 30% | −4% (size-down reduces demand here) | 26% | **8 units** |
| **XL** | 18% | −6% (less size-down crossover) | 14% | **3 units** |
| | | **Total** | **100%** | **30 units** |

#### Final Size Curve: **7 – 12 – 8 – 3**

> **Merchandiser's Rule:** M is the commercial center of gravity for this market. It is both the most populated size and the one most likely to receive size-down demand from L buyers. Never under-allocate M. If you are forced to choose between over-allocating M vs. L, always over-allocate M.

> **On XL:** Only 3 units signals exclusivity, not exclusion. However, your XL units should be *identical quality* and listed first as *available* in your pre-drop communication. A patron who knows their size is stocked — even in small numbers — will activate faster.

## PART II: THE THREE-LOOK ARCHITECTURE

### 2.1 Look Classification

The 30 units are deployed across three lifestyle contexts. Each Look is not merely a styling direction — it is a **customer acquisition channel** with distinct psychology, visual language, and purchase urgency.

| Look | Context | Buyer Archetype | Purchase Trigger | Styling Anchor |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Café** | Weekend, creative industry, social brunch | The Cultural Architect | Peer visibility, "seen wearing" | Relaxed wastra layer, visible RL Fold |
| **Boardroom** | Executive presentations, client meetings | The Boardroom Modernist | Professional authority, quiet status signal | Structured mono outfit, RL Fold as collar focus |
| **Kondangan** | Wedding receptions, cultural celebrations | The Social Curator | Social occasion, photo-worthiness | Formal wastra, elevated accessories, full RL Fold presence |

---

### 2.2 Volume Allocation by Look

#### The Governing Principle: Staged Sell-Out

The goal is not for all three looks to sell simultaneously. The goal is for each look to **exhaust its units in sequence**, creating a rolling "sold out" signal across the drop window.

- **Kondangan units sell fastest** — social occasion urgency is the strongest purchase trigger in the Indonesian market. A kondangan date is fixed; a buyer cannot defer.
- **Boardroom units sell second** — professional buyers act on considered decisions but within short windows, especially when units are limited.
- **Café units sell last** — the most lifestyle-driven purchase, lowest urgency. These units extend the "drop is still live" window, but must be small enough to clear completely.

The sequenced sell-out creates a **three-act sold-out narrative** visible to anyone watching your Instagram Stories or WhatsApp status updates.

| **TOTAL** | **30 units** | 100% | S: 7 / M: 12 / L: 8 / XL: 3 | Complete sell-through target: 72 hours |

## PART IV: THE PSYCHOLOGICAL SELL-OUT ARCHITECTURE

### 4.1 The Drop Timeline

The 72-hour window is divided into three phases, one per Look. Your communication schedule should mirror this cadence.

---

#### Hour 0–24: **Kondangan Wave** *(12 units)*

**Communication cadence:**
- **Hour 0 (Drop Open):** Send WhatsApp link to waitlist. Only show Kondangan imagery. Caption: *"Drop is open. Kondangan silhouette first. 12 units available."*
- **Hour 6:** Post Instagram Stories update: *"Kondangan — [X] units remaining."*
- **Hour 16–20:** If ≥8 units sold, post: *"Kondangan is nearly sold out. Boardroom drops in [hours]."* This activates latent Boardroom buyers who may defer.
- **Hour 24:** Announce Kondangan sold out (target). Transition to Boardroom release with a dedicated post.

> **Key Tactic — The Pre-Announcement:** 48 hours before drop, send a WhatsApp broadcast to your VIP inner circle. Do not include a purchase link. Only say: *"Kondangan goes first. 12 units. The link opens [date/time]."* This creates a mental reservation that functions like a purchase commitment.

---

#### Hour 24–48: **Boardroom Wave** *(11 units)*

**Communication cadence:**
- **Hour 24:** Boardroom imagery drops. Caption: *"Kondangan is gone. Boardroom is now open."*
- **Hour 30:** Show the RL Fold collar detail in a short video. This is the Boardroom buyer's primary decision point — they are buying authority, and the fold is the signal.
- **Hour 40:** Size availability update. Flag if M is limited: *"Boardroom M — 2 remaining."*
- **Hour 48:** Target Boardroom sold out. Transition to Café.

> **Key Tactic — The Scarcity Gradient:** Never announce how many units *remain* unless it triggers urgency (fewer than 4). Announce how many are *gone* to create momentum: *"7 of 11 Boardroom units have found their Patron."*

---

#### Hour 48–72: **Café Wave** *(7 units)*

**Communication cadence:**
- **Hour 48:** Café tone shift — warmer, more editorial. Caption: *"Only the Café edition remains. 7 pieces. This is the final chapter of Drop 01."*
- **Hour 60:** If fewer than 4 remain, activate a final 12-hour countdown post.
- **Hour 72:** Complete sell-out announcement. Archive the drop with a "Drop 01: Sold In Full" Story Highlight.

> **Key Tactic — The Closing Ritual:** When the final unit sells, post a single black image with white text: *"Drop 01. 30 Pieces. 72 Hours. It's done."* This image becomes the historical proof point for Drop 02's waitlist urgency.

---

### 4.2 The Sold-Out Psychology: Why This Works

There are three psychological mechanisms being activated simultaneously:

| Mechanism | How It's Triggered | Effect |
|---|---|---|
| **Sequential Scarcity** | Each look releases separately, so the drop "runs out" three times instead of once | Creates multiple urgency spikes rather than a single event |
| **Social Proof by Association** | "Kondangan sold out" tells Boardroom buyer that other premium women wanted this item | Reduces buyer hesitation |
| **FOMO Amplification** | Size-specific sell-out announcements (*"M is gone"*) force fence-sitters to act or lose their size permanently | Converts passive watchers to buyers |

## PART V: CONTINGENCY PROTOCOLS

### 5.1 If Kondangan Does Not Sell Through by Hour 24

Do not extend the window silently. Instead:
1. Send a targeted re-message to **only the XL and L Kondangan buyers** — often the larger sizes have the most latent demand from buyers who feared stocking out.
2. Check if the issue is price resistance (unusual for your buyer) or link friction (WhatsApp ordering process too complex). Diagnose before extending.
3. Move the Boardroom drop to Hour 36 instead of Hour 24, buying 12 more hours of Kondangan urgency.

### 5.2 If a Size Sells Out Faster Than Expected

This is a success signal, not a problem. Immediately:
1. Post a size-specific announcement: *"Kondangan M — Closed."*
2. Redirect remaining Kondangan M buyers to the Boardroom M option by messaging: *"Kondangan M is gone. Boardroom M carries the same RL Fold structure in a different configuration — and 5 remain."*

### 5.3 If You Have Unsold Units After 72 Hours

This document is designed to prevent this, but if it happens:
- **Do not discount.** A discounted RL Fold piece is a brand-integrity liability.
- Hold the remaining units for a **"One Last Piece" private message** to your most engaged 5 VIP patrons. Frame it as a *personal reservation*, not a clearance.
- If still unsold after 2 private outreaches, transfer to a **photoshoot reserve** — use the pieces for Drop 02 editorial content. They are now an asset, not dead inventory.
