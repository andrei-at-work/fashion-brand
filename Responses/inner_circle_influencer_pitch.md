# THE INNER CIRCLE: INFLUENCER COLLAB PROPOSAL
### *Rosemary Legoh × Micro-Influencer Cluster Strategy*
**Prepared by:** Brand Strategy Division  
**Date:** March 2026  
**Campaign Objective:** Create a targeted "Cluster Effect" within the urban Wastra/Modest niche through authentic early-access partnerships.

---

## STRATEGIC FRAMEWORK

This is **not** a paid-ad campaign. This is a **Design Patronage Program** — five carefully selected voices are invited to be among the first in Indonesia to wear and experience the **RL Fold**. The ask: an honest, detailed Style Review from an insider's perspective.

**Why micro-influencers (10k–50k)?**
The 10k–50k follower range is the sweet spot for Rosemary Legoh's strategy:
- **Trust Index is higher** — their audiences treat recommendations as peer opinions, not advertisements.
- **Comment-to-follower ratio** is 3–5× higher than mega-influencers, meaning active community dialogue.
- **Niche clustering** — five voices in the same sub-culture create overlapping reach, making the RL Fold feel like an "insider discovery" rather than a broadcast.

---

## THE FIVE INFLUENCER PROFILES TO TARGET

### Profile 1: The Wastra Intellectual *(Cultural Narrator)*
- **Follower Range:** 25k–45k
- **Platform:** Instagram + TikTok
- **Content Style:** Long-form Reels explaining the history and craft behind Indonesian textiles. Followers come for education, stay for aesthetic curation.
- **Target Archetype:** A content creator based in Jakarta or Yogyakarta who regularly posts about Batik philosophy, ATBM weaving, and slow fashion.
- **Why They Matter:** Their audience is pre-educated. They do not need to be convinced that the RL Fold is significant — they will immediately understand it. Their review carries the weight of academic credibility.
- **Example Search Tags:** `#BatikindonesiaDeep` `#wastrastories` `#slowfashionid`

---

### Profile 2: The Boardroom Modest Style Creator *(Professional Aesthetic Voice)*
- **Follower Range:** 20k–40k
- **Platform:** Instagram
- **Content Style:** "What I wore to [client pitch / board meeting / panel talk]" content. Modest dressing framed through a lens of professional power and intentional style.
- **Target Archetype:** A founder, consultant, or creative professional who has built an audience around the idea that modest fashion is not a compromise — it is architecture.
- **Why They Matter:** Their audience are exactly Rosemary Legoh's buyers. The RL Fold's asymmetric collar structure photographs powerfully in boardroom-adjacent content — it reads as sculpture, not costume.
- **Example Search Tags:** `#modestOOTD` `#hijanstyle` `#indonesianworkwear`

---

### Profile 3: The Kondangan Curator *(Celebration Dressing Authority)*
- **Follower Range:** 15k–35k
- **Platform:** Instagram + Pinterest
- **Content Style:** Indonesian wedding and celebration looks — but curated with taste, not excess. They showcase how to be dressed "appropriately elevated" in cultural settings.
- **Target Archetype:** A stylist, fashion editor, or well-dressed wedding guest who documents their kondangan journey with care.
- **Why They Matter:** The kondangan market in Indonesia is enormous, but most Wastra worn to these events is indistinguishable. The RL Fold as a kondangan outer is a disruptor — it honors cultural context while refusing to disappear into the crowd.
- **Example Search Tags:** `#kondanganOOTD` `#hajatandress` `#batikformal`

---

### Profile 4: The Jakarta Art Scene Insider *(Cultural Capital Voice)*
- **Follower Range:** 10k–25k
- **Platform:** Instagram + Substack or Blog
- **Content Style:** Gallery openings, cultural events, creative industry gatherings. Their posts are a visual diary of Jakarta's design-literate underground.
- **Target Archetype:** A gallerist, art consultant, curator, or creative director who is more interested in the concept behind a garment than its price tag.
- **Why They Matter:** Their audience does not follow trends — they set them. An RL Fold worn to an art opening in Kemang or Dia.Lo.Gue will be photographed by people who matter. This is how "iconic" moments are manufactured.
- **Example Search Tags:** `#jakartaartscene` `#jakartacreative` `#indonesiandesign`

---

### Profile 5: The Diaspora Modernist *(International Legitimacy Amplifier)*
- **Follower Range:** 12k–30k
- **Platform:** Instagram + YouTube Shorts
- **Content Style:** An Indonesian living abroad (Singapore, Netherlands, Australia, UAE) who curates their cultural identity through clothing. Posts about "wearing Indonesia with modern confidence."
- **Target Archetype:** A diaspora professional or creative who actively documents the experience of being Indonesian in global cities, using fashion as a bridge between cultures.
- **Why They Matter:** International reach gives Rosemary Legoh aspirational gravity — domestic buyers want what global Indonesians are wearing. This is also a direct pipeline to international stockist conversations in the future.
- **Example Search Tags:** `#indonesianabroad` `#globalindonesian` `#modernwastra`

## CREATING THE CLUSTER EFFECT: TIMING ARCHITECTURE

The five pieces must not arrive at the same time. The timing is engineered to create a **ripple, not a splash**.

| Day | Action | Effect |
|-----|--------|--------|
| **Day 1** | Profile 1 (Wastra Intellectual) receives piece + posts "something is coming" teaser | Seeds intellectual curiosity |
| **Day 3** | Profile 4 (Art Scene Insider) wears to an event, posts candid | Street-level credibility without announcement |
| **Day 5** | Profile 2 (Boardroom Modest) posts full review — "What I wore to [meeting]" | Professional authority validates the piece |
| **Day 7** | Profile 3 (Kondangan Curator) posts "waiting for my ceremony look" teaser | Celebration niche activated |
| **Day 9** | Profile 5 (Diaspora Modernist) posts from abroad — "Jakarta sent me this" | International legitimacy spike |
| **Day 10** | Waitlist link drops to private list | Audience has already been primed by 5 trusted voices |

**Result:** By the time the waitlist link opens, the RL Fold already has earned cultural context from five different communities. It is not a product launch. It is an *arrival*.

## WHAT TO SEND WITH THE PIECE

Each early-access package should include:

1. **The Garment** — folded and wrapped in tissue, placed inside the signature box.
2. **The Heritage Note** — a small card that reads:
   > *"The RL Fold is not decoration. It is architecture. Wiron pleating meets modernist structure — made for a woman who understands both."*
3. **The Design Sheet** — A single printed card (no logo, no price) showing the fold geometry from three angles with handwritten annotations.
4. **A Personal Line** — One sentence handwritten by Rosemary specifically for this person. Not generic. ("Your work on Batik Pesisir storytelling is why this piece belongs with you first.")

---

## WHAT NOT TO DO

- **Do not send a rate card.** This is not a paid campaign — attaching money cheapens the gesture and the garment.
- **Do not send a creative brief.** Trust the influencer's voice. A brief turns a patron into a vendor.
- **Do not follow up asking when they will post.** Patience signals confidence in the product.
- **Do not send to more than 5.** Scarcity has to be real. If ten people received "early access," it is not inner circle — it is a PR blast.

---

## MEASURING THE CLUSTER EFFECT

**Metrics that matter:**
| Signal | What It Means |
|--------|---------------|
| One influencer's audience DMs another influencer asking about the fold | The cluster is working — audiences are crossing |
| A follower of Profile 4 follows Profile 1 because of the RL Fold connection | Sub-culture convergence is happening |
| Someone tags Rosemary Legoh with the caption "I've been waiting for this" | Pre-desire exists before the drop |
| A fashion editor or journalist reaches out after seeing multiple posts | Earned media triggered by cluster density |

**Metrics that don't matter (for this stage):**
- Total reach or impressions
- Follower growth from these posts
- Sales directly attributed to any one influencer

The goal at this stage is **cultural embedding**, not conversion tracking.

---

## LONG-TERM INNER CIRCLE STRUCTURE

Once the first drop cycle is complete, formalize the relationship:

- **Title:** *Rosemary Legoh Patron of the First Fold* (informal, used in private communications and inner-circle packaging inserts)
- **Access:** First-look invitations to all future drops before any public announcement
- **Recognition:** Named in a "Founding Patrons" section of the brand's eventual website — a permanent record of who believed first
- **Expectation:** No contractual obligation. Only the expectation that they continue to wear and respond honestly if moved to do so.

This is how you build a **brand inner circle** that lasts beyond a single campaign. The influencers become ambassadors not because they were paid, but because they were *chosen first*.
