## PART I: FOUNDATIONAL PHILOSOPHY

### 1.1 The Structural Demand of the RL Fold

The **RL Fold** is not a decorative element — it is a load-bearing architectural gesture. Unlike standard flat-pattern batik construction, the RL Fold imposes three simultaneous demands on any fabric selected for Rosemary Legoh signature outers:

1. **Crease Memory** — The cloth must hold a fold line under pressure and return to it after movement without professional re-pressing.
2. **Tensile Resilience** — The diagonal fold axis places anisotropic stress on the weave structure; at this bias point, yarns must not separate or distort over repeated wearings.
3. **Drape Directionality** — The asymmetric cascade below the fold must fall in a single, intentional plane — not collapse, flare, or "bubble" at the unseamed edge.

A fabric that satisfies conventional batik applications — display-worthy, photogenic, culturally significant — may be entirely unsuitable for the RL Fold. This manual establishes the minimum performance thresholds a textile must meet before it is approved for Rosemary Legoh production.

---

### 1.2 The "Wastra Intelligence" Principle

Rosemary Legoh operates within a strict **Wastra Intelligence** sourcing philosophy:

- **Origin Traceability:** Every fabric used carries a documented provenance — the workshop, the master weaver (*pengrajin*), and the motif's cultural classification.
- **Exclusivity Covenant:** No motif approved for use in a Rosemary Legoh collection may simultaneously appear in mass-production retail or third-party label runs.
- **Living Craft Alignment:** Sourcing partnerships prioritize workshops that employ traditional hand-production methods, ensuring Rosemary Legoh's economics directly sustain artisan livelihoods.

## PART II: THE SOURCING NETWORK

### 2.1 Primary Sourcing Hubs

#### Solo (Surakarta) — *The Court of Structured Motifs*

Solo batik is distinguished by its **sogan palette** (brown-cream earth tones derived from the *soga* tree bark) and its adherence to royal Javanese court motifs — *parang*, *kawung*, *truntum*. These motifs are characterized by:

- **Geometric precision** — repeating units with hard, defined edges
- **High visual density** — motifs are traditionally closely packed
- **Cultural gravity** — solo motifs carry aristocratic lineage and are recognized as prestige signals by Rosemary Legoh's core clientele

**Structural suitability:** Solo batik on **cotton primissima** (the highest grade of Javanese batik cotton) exhibits excellent crease memory due to its tightly woven, low-stretch construction. This makes it the **primary substrate candidate** for the RL Fold collar.

**Recommended partner profile:**
- Workshops operating in the *Laweyan* or *Kauman* batik districts
- Minimum 2-generation master weaver lineage
- Capacity to produce exclusive "closed motifs" — adaptations of traditional designs sealed by a naming agreement that prevents reproduction for any other label

---

#### Pekalongan — *The Laboratory of Textile Innovation*

Pekalongan batik (*batik pesisir*, or coastal batik) is structurally distinct from Solo. Influenced by centuries of Chinese, Dutch, and Arab trade, its characteristics include:

- **Looser, more expressive motifs** — florals, fauna, and hybrid cultural imagery
- **Brighter, multi-color palettes** — suitable for editorial and occasion-wear lines
- **Higher innovation culture** — Pekalongan workshops are more accustomed to custom color requests and non-standard fabric weights

**Structural suitability:** Pekalongan workshops frequently work with **viscose-cotton blends**, **ATBM (Alat Tenun Bukan Mesin)** woven silk, and **mori batik** bases. The fluidity of these textiles makes them candidates for the **cascade drape** portion of RL Fold construction — not the primary fold structure itself, but the outer shell panel where movement is desired.

**Recommended partner profile:**
- Workshops on *Jl. Blimbing* corridor or affiliated with the Pekalongan Batik Museum's artisan registry
- Demonstrable experience executing custom dye colorways (Pantone reference-matched)
- Minimum stock capacity of 80–120 meters per exclusive motif run

---

#### Secondary Sourcing: ATBM Woven Textiles (Tenun)

For structured outers requiring greater rigidity — particularly for the horizontal fold arm of the RL Fold collar — **handwoven tenun** from:

- **Tenun Troso** (Jepara, Central Java) — dense, structured weave with high body
- **Tenun Lurik** (Klaten, Central Java) — a striped ikat tradition with exceptional tensile strength

These textiles are not traditional batik but are **Wastra-classified** and qualify under Rosemary Legoh's heritage sourcing mandate. Their structural density makes them ideal for **interlining-free collar construction**, reducing production complexity.

### 2.2 Establishing the Exclusive Motif Partnership

The following is the Rosemary Legoh standard process for commissioning an **Exclusive Partner Motif**:

#### Step 1 — The Heritage Consultation (*Musyawarah Motif*)
Schedule a minimum 2-session consultation with the master pengrajin (artisan). Session 1: share the brand's visual language — architectural references, geometric vocabulary, the RL Fold's diagonal axis. Session 2: review the artisan's proposed adaptation of a motif lineage. Both parties align on a "departure point" — the traditional motif that will be evolved, not erased.

#### Step 2 — The Exclusivity Agreement
Draft a written **Motif Exclusivity Covenant** in Bahasa Indonesia (legally binding) and English (reference copy) that specifies:
- The motif name and its documented cultural lineage
- The exclusive production period (minimum 24 months per collection cycle)
- Prohibition on sale of the same motif to any competing label, online marketplace, or export aggregator
- Rosemary Legoh's right to co-credit the artisan on garment labels ("*Motif by [Workshop Name], [City]*")

#### Step 3 — Sampling & Structural Testing
Commission a **minimum 5-meter sample run** before any production commitment. This sample must pass all tests in the **RL Fold Drape & Rigidity Checklist** (see Part III) before the exclusivity fee is paid and production begins.

#### Step 4 — Living Rate Agreement
Pricing is set at a minimum of **120% above local market rate** for equivalent non-exclusive fabric. This premium signals to the artisan that the partnership is an investment — not a cost-compression exercise. Payment terms: 40% upon motif approval, 60% upon delivery.

#### Step 5 — Attribution & Documentation
Photograph the artisan, their workshop, and the motif-making process. This content is used in Rosemary Legoh's **Brand Transparency Archive** and shared with clients who receive the garment — connecting buyer to maker in a documented human chain.

## PART III: THE RL FOLD DRAPE & RIGIDITY CHECKLIST

*This checklist must be completed on every fabric sample BEFORE production approval. Testing must be performed on a sewn test collar constructed to the actual RL Fold pattern — not on raw yardage alone.*

---

### SECTION A: BASE FABRIC QUALIFICATION

| # | Test | Method | Pass Threshold |
|---|------|---------|----------------|
| A1 | **Thread Count / Weave Density** | Count threads per cm under a loupe | Minimum 40 threads/cm (both warp and weft) for cotton; 35/cm for silk blends |
| A2 | **Shrinkage Pre-test** | Wash swatch at 30°C, measure before/after | Maximum 3% shrinkage in any direction |
| A3 | **Bias Stretch** | Cut swatch on 45° angle, apply 500g hanging weight for 2 hours | Maximum 4mm elongation per 30cm |
| A4 | **Dye Fastness (Rub)** | Dry and wet crock test per ISO 105-X12 | Minimum Grade 4 (dry), Grade 3 (wet) |
| A5 | **Dye Fastness (Light)** | Expose to 3 hours direct sun; compare to grey scale | Minimum Grade 3–4 |
| A6 | **Motif Registration Accuracy** | Measure repeat unit across 3 meters of fabric | Maximum 2mm deviation per repeat unit |

---

### SECTION B: FOLD INTEGRITY TESTS

*A sewn RL Fold test collar is required for all tests in this section.*

| # | Test | Method | Pass Threshold |
|---|------|---------|----------------|
| B1 | **Crease Memory (Initial)** | Press fold with iron at appropriate fabric temp, hold 10 seconds. Hang vertical for 2 hours. | Fold angle must retain ≥ 85% of pressed shape after hanging |
| B2 | **Crease Memory (Post-Wear Simulation)** | Crease fabric 50 times along fold axis by hand, rehang. | Fold line must remain visually distinct; no fraying or weave separation at crease |
| B3 | **Structural Collapse Test** | Drape test collar on dress form; apply lateral pressure of 200g to outer fold arm for 30 seconds. Release. | Collar must return to original asymmetric position without manual re-adjustment |
| B4 | **Diagonal Seam Stress Test** | Sew a 15cm seam on 45° bias, apply 1kg pull force for 60 seconds | No seam rupture; maximum 1mm seam slippage |
| B5 | **Cascade Drape Uniformity** | Hang completed test collar from neckline fixing points; photograph from front, 45°, and side | Cascade panel must fall in a single plane; no lateral "ballooning" or uneven edge lift |
| B6 | **Interlining Compatibility** | Bond approved interfacing (woven, medium weight) to collar panel; press and fold | No bubbling, separation, or stiffness inconsistency after bonding |

---

### SECTION C: MULTI-WEARING DURABILITY TESTS

*Simulate a client wearing the garment to 3 events over 30 days.*

| # | Test | Method | Pass Threshold |
|---|------|---------|----------------|
| C1 | **Post-Storage Recovery** | Fold garment flat (as if stored in packaging), store for 72 hours, open and hang for 1 hour | RL Fold collar must recover to visible structural form without pressing |
| C2 | **Post-Hand-Wash Integrity** | Gentle hand wash, hang dry, assess fold region | Fabric must not distort; motif registration must hold; crease line must be recoverable with light pressing |
| C3 | **Seating Stress** | Test on a live model who sits for 3 hours; assess fold collar region on rising | Asymmetric fold geometry must require no more than 1 light press with palm to restore |
| C4 | **Edge Fraying (Over Time)** | Examine raw unseamed edge of cascade panel under loupe after 3 wash cycles | No more than 0.5mm fraying beyond seam allowance |
| C5 | **Motif Alignment at Seam** | After construction and washing, examine seam points where motif crosses the fold line | No more than 1.5mm motif misalignment at seam intersections |

---

### SECTION D: SUSTAINABILITY & ETHICS COMPLIANCE

| # | Criterion | Verification Method | Standard |
|---|-----------|---------------------|----------|
| D1 | **Natural Dye Proportion** | Lab certificate or artisan declaration | Minimum 40% natural/plant-based dye content for "Heritage" label items |
| D2 | **Wastewater Handling** | Site visit or third-party certification | Workshop must have documented dye wastewater containment; no direct river discharge |
| D3 | **Artisan Wage Verification** | Payroll review or cooperative audit | All artisans paid minimum 130% of regional UMK (Upah Minimum Kabupaten) |
| D4 | **Child Labor Prohibition** | Workshop visitation and documentation | Zero under-18 workers; confirmed in writing |
| D5 | **Fabric Origin Documentation** | Certificate of thread origin | All base cloth (*mori*, *primissima*, silk) must carry mill-of-origin documentation |

## PART IV: APPROVED FABRIC MATRIX

The following fabric categories are approved for specific structural roles in the RL Fold construction. No substitutions without full re-testing.

| Fabric Type | Source Region | Structural Role | RL Fold Application |
|-------------|--------------|-----------------|---------------------|
| **Cotton Primissima** (batik) | Solo (Laweyan/Kauman) | High crease memory, low stretch | Primary fold collar structure |
| **Mori Batik** (fine cotton) | Pekalongan | Moderate body, good drape | Cascade outer panel |
| **Tenun Troso** | Jepara | Very high rigidity, geometric texture | Statement collar on non-batik line |
| **ATBM Woven Silk** | Pekalongan / Palembang | High luster, moderate structure | Evening/formal outer (fold with lining) |
| **Tenun Lurik** | Klaten | Exceptional tensile strength | Structural lining for RL Fold collar |
| **Viscose-Cotton Blend** | Pekalongan workshops | Soft drape, color vividness | Fashion-forward seasonal prints only |

> **Prohibited Fabrics:** Polyester blends above 20%, knit constructions, and any non-woven bonded textiles are permanently disqualified from RL Fold collar use. They do not hold a crease line and will compromise the structural identity of the garment.

## PART V: SUSTAINABILITY COMMITMENTS

### 5.1 The Rosemary Legoh Fabric Pledge

Every Rosemary Legoh exclusive outer garment is produced under the following non-negotiable commitments:

1. **Zero anonymous sourcing.** Every fabric carries a documented origin with a named workshop and a named artisan where possible.
2. **Fair rate minimums.** Sourcing rates are never compressed to optimize margin. The 65% gross margin target is achieved through price architecture — not artisan cost-cutting.
3. **Motif sovereignty.** Rosemary Legoh will never use, adapt, or commission a sacred cultural motif (*motif larangan*) without explicit consultation with the relevant cultural authority or royal household.
4. **Archive commitment.** For every exclusive motif produced, the brand contributes one documented fabric sample to the artisan's own archive — preserving the collaboration in the maker's history, not just the brand's.
5. **Production lot limits.** Maximum 30 units per exclusive motif run per season. This protects both scarcity for the brand and production dignity for the artisan — no one is running an assembly line.

---

### 5.2 The "Craft Continuity" Investment Model

For long-term partner workshops, Rosemary Legoh commits to a **minimum 3-drop annual engagement** per year — not a transactional one-time purchase. This signals to the artisan that the partnership justifies investment in the design adaptation process, training junior craftspeople on the exclusive motif, and maintaining the equipment needed to meet Rosemary Legoh's structural standards.

This model transforms Rosemary Legoh from a *buyer* into a **patron of the craft** — a positioning that is both ethical and strategically powerful. It is extraordinarily difficult for a copycat brand operating on transactional sourcing to replicate a fabric that was co-designed with an artisan who is contractually, financially, and creatively invested in your exclusivity.

## PART VI: FABRIC APPROVAL SIGN-OFF PROTOCOL

Before any fabric enters production, the following sign-off chain must be completed:

```
[ ] Fabric Sample Received (5m minimum)
[ ] Section A Checklist Complete — Pass/Fail recorded
[ ] Section B Checklist Complete — Pass/Fail recorded
[ ] Section C Multi-Wear Test Complete — Pass/Fail recorded
[ ] Section D Sustainability Compliance Verified
[ ] Motif Exclusivity Covenant signed (if new partner motif)
[ ] Artisan Attribution documentation collected
[ ] Production Briefing issued to Pattern Cutter with approved fabric spec sheet
[ ] Final approval signed: Rosemary Legoh (Designer)
```

> *No fabric proceeds to cutting without a complete sign-off. A partial checklist is a failed checklist.*

## APPENDIX A: KEY TERMS

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Wastra** | A collective term for Indonesian traditional woven and printed textiles, encompassing batik, tenun, and ikat traditions |
| **Pengrajin** | A skilled artisan or craftsperson; in this context, a master batik maker or weaver |
| **Primissima** | The highest grade of Javanese batik cotton — tightly woven, low shrinkage, superior crease tolerance |
| **Mori** | The base white cotton cloth on which batik is applied; quality grades range from mori kasar (coarse) to mori primissima (finest) |
| **Wiron/Wiru** | Traditional accordion-pleating technique used in Javanese court dress; the ancestral lineage of the RL Fold |
| **ATBM** | *Alat Tenun Bukan Mesin* — a non-motorized handloom; fabric produced on ATBM carries higher craft value and exclusivity |
| **UMK** | *Upah Minimum Kabupaten* — the minimum regional wage set per district in Indonesia |
| **Motif Larangan** | Literally "forbidden motif" — sacred or royal-lineage patterns (e.g., *Parang Rusak*) restricted from commercial use without cultural authorization |
| **Sogan** | The characteristic brown-cream earth-tone palette of Solo batik, derived from *soga* bark dye |
