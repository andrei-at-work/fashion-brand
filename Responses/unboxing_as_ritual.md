## PART I: DESIGN PHILOSOPHY — THE PACKAGE AS PROLOGUE

Before a customer sees the **RL Fold**, she must feel it. The unboxing experience for **Rosemary Legoh** is not logistics — it is a **designed ceremony**. The moment of arrival must communicate everything the brand stands for: restraint, depth, structural intelligence, and the quiet confidence of something made by human hands.

The packaging system is governed by three axioms:

1. **Structural Beauty Before Surface Beauty** — Form and material must be tactilely premium before anything is printed on them. The box must feel like the garment before it is opened.
2. **Sustainable Luxury is Not a Compromise** — Materials are chosen because they are *more* refined than synthetic alternatives, not despite being ecological.
3. **The Unboxing is a Shareable Story** — Every element inside is a prop in a moment that a customer will want to record and share. Nothing is filler. Everything has a narrative function.

## PART II: THE PACKAGING ARCHITECTURE

### 2.1 The Outer Vessel — The "Gedung" Box

**Form Factor:** Rigid two-piece lift-off box (no magnetic snap — the deliberate, silent lift of the lid is the first ritual moment).

**Dimensions:** 38 cm × 38 cm × 12 cm (slightly generous; the garment is not compressed, it is *presented*).

**Material Specification:**

| Component | Material | Finish |
|---|---|---|
| Outer shell | 3mm rigid greyboard, wrapped in **Kraft-linen laminate** | Uncoated, matte, slightly textured — resembling architectural concrete |
| Inner shell | 2mm greyboard, lined with **natural undyed cotton muslin** | Soft, breathable, scent-neutral |
| Lid exterior | Single debossed wordmark: **ROSEMARY LEGOH** | Set in a narrow sans-serif; pressed 0.5mm deep — no foil, no color |
| Lid underside | A single line of copy printed in warm grey ink | *"Crafted in Small Batches. Made by Hand. Held by You."* |

**Sustainability Note:** Greyboard is FSC-certified recycled content. The Kraft-linen laminate is water-based adhesive bonded (no solvent glue). The inner muslin lining is sourced from the same Solo and Pekalongan weaving cooperatives that supply the garment fabric — a closed-loop material story.

**Color:** The box exterior is a single, deliberate tone: **Tanah Muda** — a warm off-white with the faintest grey undertone. Not stark white (which reads clinical). Not cream (which reads vintage). Tanah Muda reads as *unbuilt material* — raw, considered, waiting.

---

### 2.2 The Inner Layer — The "Lapisan" Reveal System

The interior is not loose tissue stuffed into corners. It is a **three-stage reveal** engineered to slow the customer down and build anticipation.

**Stage 1 — The Sealing Band**

Draped across the folded garment is a **12 cm wide band of handwoven Lurik fabric**, in the brand's signature two-color stripe: **Tanah (warm off-white) and Laut (deep ocean indigo)**. This band is tied — not glued, not stapled — in a simple, flat knot at the center, directly above the RL Fold on the garment, so the customer's first point of contact is the knot and the fold simultaneously.

> *This is the first functional encounter with the brand's structural logic: untying the band is the first act of inhabiting a Rosemary Legoh piece.*

The Lurik band is not disposable. It is finished on both edges with a rolled hem. Customers are instructed on the Heritage Note (see Section 2.3) that it can be repurposed as a bookmark, a hair tie, or a gift-wrapping ribbon.

**Stage 2 — The Garment Layer**

The garment is folded in a single, precise manner defined in the Quality Assurance protocol: the **RL Fold collar is placed face-up at the top**, centered. The fold is never obscured. It is the first visual element revealed when the Lurik band is untied.

The garment sits directly on the muslin lining, unbagged. No plastic polybag. No silica gel. The muslin breathes; it does not trap odor or humidity. The garment is the hero — it is not sheathed.

**Stage 3 — The Foundation Layer**

Beneath the garment, flat against the muslin, are two final elements:
- The **Heritage Note** (see Section 2.3)
- A small envelope containing a **Wax Seal Card** (see Section 2.4)

---

### 2.3 The Heritage Note — *"Secarik Sejarah"*

**Physical Specs:** 10 cm × 15 cm. Printed on **300 gsm uncoated stone paper** (calcium carbonate-based, zero wood pulp, water-resistant). Portrait orientation. Single fold — opens like a small book.

**Cover (Outside):** The Abstract Monogram (The Mark) — letterpress printed in warm indigo. Below it, in a small italic serif: *"A note on your piece."*

**Inside Left Panel — The Garment Identity:**

```
Piece No.     [XX / 30]
Collection    [Season Name]
Colorway      [Tanah Muda / Laut Dalam / etc.]
Fabric Origin [Pekalongan Batik Cap, Solo Lurik, etc.]
Artisan       [Name or Studio Collective]
Completed     [Month, Year]
```

*This panel positions the garment as an object with provenance — like a work of art, not a unit of inventory.*

**Inside Right Panel — The RL Fold Story:**

> *"The fold you are holding was made by hand.*
>
> *It draws from two traditions: the wiron pleat of Javanese court dress — a precise, repetitive accordion fold that has dressed royalty for centuries — and the structural origami logic of modernist architecture, where a single diagonal crease transforms a flat plane into a three-dimensional form.*
>
> *The RL Fold bridges these two intelligences. It is asymmetric by design: one side rises, the other falls. It cannot be sewn by machine. It requires a tailor who understands both textile behavior and spatial geometry.*
>
> *This is not decoration. This is your garment's architecture.*
>
> *— Rosemary Legoh*"

**Back Panel (Outside):** Simple care instructions in two languages (Indonesian and English), printed in a light warm grey. Below the care symbols, a small line: *"This note is printed on stone paper. It is waterproof and can be kept indefinitely."*

---

### 2.4 The Wax Seal Card — *"Tanda Pribadi"*

**Physical Specs:** 6 cm × 9 cm. Heavyweight cotton rag card, 400 gsm. Ecru tone.

**Content:** A hand-applied wax seal in **deep indigo** bearing The Mark (the brand's Abstract Monogram). Below the seal, handwritten in ink by the Rosemary Legoh studio team:

> *"Piece [XX] of [30] — Thank you for being part of this circle."*
> *[Signed: Rosemary]*

**Notes on Execution:**

- The wax seal is applied by a single team member at the studio, not outsourced.
- The handwriting does not need to be identical across pieces — variance is a feature, not a flaw. It proves the human hand is present.
- At a production volume of 30 units per drop, this adds approximately **45–60 minutes** to the packing workflow and costs almost nothing. The psychological value for the customer is disproportionately high.

## PART III: THE OUTER SHIPPING LAYER

**The brand box is never shipped naked inside a plain brown courier box.** This is a moment of brand control that most boutiques abandon — and it is a mistake.

**Outer Mailer:** A collapsible rigid mailer (telescoping flat), manufactured in the same Kraft-linen material as the box, printed with a single line on the exterior: *"Handle with the care of something made by hand."* This is the first message a customer reads when the courier delivers the package.

**Void Fill:** Zero plastic peanuts, zero bubble wrap. The brand box fits snugly within the outer mailer by design (a custom 2mm clearance tolerance on all sides, filled with a single sheet of crinkled **recycled natural Kraft tissue**). The tissue is shredded in a single direction — not randomly — so it resembles the layered wiron pleat when pulled apart.

**Fragrance:** A single drop of the brand's designated scent oil — a warm blend of **gaharu (agarwood) and white sandalwood** — is applied to the inner surface of the outer mailer flap before sealing. The customer smells the brand before she sees the inner box. This is a subliminal identity marker. Over time, the scent becomes a trigger for brand recall.

## PART IV: THE SOCIAL MEDIA-WORTHY MOMENT

### 4.1 Engineering the "Shareable Arc"

The unboxing video has become a primary form of word-of-mouth marketing. The Rosemary Legoh unboxing is engineered to produce three distinct "peak moments" that are visually compelling and naturally clip-worthy:

| Moment | Action | Visual Hook |
|---|---|---|
| **Peak 1 — The Lift** | Removing the outer mailer box, revealing the Tanah Muda brand box | Clean, architectural; the debossed wordmark catches oblique light |
| **Peak 2 — The Untie** | Untying the Lurik band to reveal the garment | Hands on woven fabric, the color story of Tanah and Laut against the white muslin; the RL Fold collar emerges |
| **Peak 3 — The Note** | Opening the Heritage Note and reading the RL Fold story | Letterpress monogram, stone paper texture, handwritten wax seal card — all in one frame |

### 4.2 Lighting Design Guidance (for Customer Content)

Include a small printed insert (back of the Wax Seal Card envelope) with the following note:

> *"For the best photography of your piece, hold it near a window with indirect natural light. The RL Fold collar is designed to catch oblique light — it will cast its own shadow geometry."*

This is not just helpful — it is coaching the customer to produce better content without telling her she is being coached.

### 4.3 The Brand Hashtag Ecosystem

Pre-print the following on the inner lid of the brand box, in the smallest legible size, centered, in warm grey:

```
#RosemaryLegoh  #RLFold  #StrukturDanWarisan
```

Do not add a call-to-action ("Tag us!"). The hashtags are present. The customer will use them if the experience earns it.

## PART V: MATERIAL COST ANALYSIS & UNIT ECONOMICS

*For a 30-unit small-batch drop:*

| Component | Estimated Unit Cost (IDR) |
|---|---|
| Rigid brand box (Kraft-linen, custom) | Rp 45,000 – 65,000 |
| Inner muslin lining | Rp 8,000 – 12,000 |
| Lurik woven sealing band (per unit, cut from roll) | Rp 15,000 – 20,000 |
| Heritage Note (stone paper, letterpress) | Rp 12,000 – 18,000 |
| Wax Seal Card + wax + handwriting time | Rp 8,000 – 12,000 |
| Outer Kraft mailer (custom print) | Rp 20,000 – 28,000 |
| Gaharu/sandalwood scent application | Rp 2,000 – 3,000 |
| Tissue fill | Rp 2,000 – 4,000 |
| **Total per unit** | **Rp 112,000 – 162,000** |

**Interpretation:** At a garment price point of Rp 1,200,000 – Rp 2,500,000+, the packaging represents approximately **6–13% of retail price** — consistent with luxury packaging benchmarks (typically 5–15%). This is not overhead to minimize. It is **customer experience investment** that directly reduces return rates, increases UGC (user-generated content), and strengthens repeat purchase intent.
